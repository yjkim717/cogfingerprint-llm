## The Weight of a Like

It's funny, isn't it? How much of our lives now feels… curated? Scrolling through Instagram, it's all sunshine and perfect avocado toast, isn't it? And I get it, we all want to present the best version of ourselves. I definitely do. I spent a good ten minutes rearranging my bookshelf the other day just for a photo. 

But lately, I’ve been feeling this weird pressure. Like, the value of a moment isn’t in *experiencing* it, but in documenting it and getting that little dopamine hit from a like. Remember when we used to just *be* somewhere, without needing to prove we were?

This year (2022) has been a year of trying to detach a little. Less posting, more *living*. It’s been surprisingly liberating. I still share things, of course – I love connecting with friends and family – but I'm trying to be more mindful about *why* I’m sharing. Is it genuine connection, or just a subconscious need for validation?

It’s a constant work in progress. I still catch myself thinking, "This would look great online!" But then I take a breath, put the phone down, and just… enjoy the moment. And honestly? Those are the moments I remember the most. The ones that don't need a filter.
