## The Credit Score and the Dream (and Maybe a Little Bit of Disappointment)

Okay, let’s be real. Apartment hunting in NYC in 2022 is… exhausting. I'm officially entering my third month of searching, and I’m starting to feel like a number more than a person. Specifically, *that* number: my credit score.

It’s wild, isn't it? You can have a steady job, a solid savings account, and a genuinely lovely personality (I'm told!), but if your credit score isn't magically perfect, forget about it. Landlords seem to operate under the assumption that a score is the be-all and end-all of your financial responsibility.

I've been thinking a lot about this lately. It feels unfair. Life happens. Unexpected expenses, student loans, those times you *really* needed to use a credit card to get you through… they all impact your score. Does that automatically mean I'm a bad renter? Absolutely not.

It's made me really examine my own financial habits. I'm diving into budgeting apps, trying to understand the system better. Maybe becoming an authorized user on my mom’s account is the next step. It’s frustrating, sure, but it’s also forcing me to level up my financial literacy. Hopefully, I'll find a place soon, and maybe, just maybe, this whole ordeal will lead to something positive in the long run.
