## The Quiet Shift: Rethinking My Crypto Dreams (and My Coffee Runs)

Okay, let's be real. 2022 has been…a lot. The markets have been wild, the news cycles relentless, and my crypto portfolio has seen more ups and downs than a rollercoaster.  I dove into crypto a couple of years ago, fueled by the promise of financial independence and the sheer excitement of being part of something revolutionary. Ethereum, in particular, felt like the future – a decentralized platform, smart contracts, the whole shebang.

I even briefly entertained the idea of using ETH for everyday purchases. Imagine, no bank fees, instant transactions! The fantasy was strong. But then reality started to creep in. 

Every time I bought a latte with ETH, I was staring down the barrel of a potential capital gains tax. Seriously, tracking every single transaction felt like a part-time job.  And the volatility?  Buying a sandwich with ETH felt like gambling – would it be worth more or less tomorrow? 

It’s made me rethink my overall strategy. I still believe in the long-term potential of crypto, and I'm certainly not selling everything. But maybe financial independence isn't about using digital assets for *everything*. Maybe it’s about strategically building a portfolio, understanding the tax implications, and recognizing that sometimes, a regular debit card for your daily coffee is just…sensible. 

I'm shifting towards a more measured approach, focusing on long-term investment assets and leaving the everyday transactions to the tried-and-true.
