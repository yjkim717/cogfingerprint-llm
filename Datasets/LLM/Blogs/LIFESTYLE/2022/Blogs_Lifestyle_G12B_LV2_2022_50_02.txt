## The Joy of Unlearning: My Kitchen, My Habits, and a Really Good Salad

It's funny, isn't it, how we get stuck in routines? I've been feeling that a lot lately. 2022 has been… a year. A year of adjustments, a year of questioning things I thought were just *how things were*. And it started, oddly enough, in my kitchen.

For years, I’ve meticulously followed recipes, measuring everything to the gram, feeling like that was the only way to achieve, you know, *results*. Baking was a science, cooking was an equation. Then, a few weeks ago, I was making a salad. A simple salad. And I realized I was reaching for a fancy vinaigrette recipe, one involving emulsification and whisking for precisely three minutes. 

Why?

I just… tossed olive oil, vinegar, and a pinch of salt together. And it was *delicious*. Better than anything I'd ever made following a recipe. 

It sparked something. Like realizing you don't *need* that complicated function in your code, you can just do it directly.  I started questioning other things: the elaborate morning skincare routine, the perfectly curated Instagram feed, the pressure to always be "doing" something.

Maybe, just maybe, simplicity is the key. Maybe the joy isn't in the complex process, but in the effortless result.  Maybe unlearning some of these habits, these expectations, is the most freeing thing I can do.

Now, I’m embracing the spontaneous salad, the messy bun, and the quiet evenings spent simply *being*. And honestly? It feels pretty good.
