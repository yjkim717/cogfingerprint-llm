It was supposed to be our year. Freshly married in 2022, we were navigating new last names and merging finances. I’d maxed my Roth IRA early, a point of pride. Then we sat down with our tax advisor. Filing jointly for the first time, our combined income pushed us over the Roth limit. The potential penalty felt like a gut punch—a classic newlywed oversight. We’re now learning the “backdoor” process, a financial lesson in communication we never saw coming. Marriage is full of surprises, isn’t it?
