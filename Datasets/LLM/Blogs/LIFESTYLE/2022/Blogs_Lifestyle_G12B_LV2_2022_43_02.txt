## The Joy of Decluttering... My Digital Life, Too

Okay, real talk: 2022 has been a year of *massive* decluttering for me. You know, the Marie Kondo method applied to my closet, the KonMari-ing of my kitchen drawers… the whole shebang. It felt so good, letting go of things that weren’t serving me.

But it got me thinking – I’d been ignoring a *huge* chunk of my life that desperately needed a spring clean: my digital world. I had years of photos scattered across various clouds, documents buried in folders I couldn’t even remember creating, and honestly, just a general feeling of digital overwhelm.

It's surprisingly similar to a physical clutter, isn't it? All this information, all these files... It can feel like sifting through a giant CSV file, desperately trying to find that *one* specific memory, that *one* important document.

I started small, creating tighter folder structures and finally tackling that pile of screenshots. It's a slow process, but the feeling of regaining control – of being able to actually *find* what I need, when I need it – is incredibly satisfying. It’s a little digital peace of mind, and honestly, it's been a game-changer for my overall sense of calm. Maybe 2023 will be the year I automate the process!
