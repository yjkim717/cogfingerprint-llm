>## The Unexpected Calm of Digital Conquest

It's funny, isn't it? How finding peace can happen in the most unexpected places. Lately, my evenings have been spent playing Europa Universalis IV, meticulously expanding the Manchu empire, gobbling up Ming provinces one by one. There's a strangely meditative quality to the strategic planning, the careful core province acquisition. After a hectic day, the calculated chaos of virtual conquest is surprisingly calming. Who knew historical grand strategy could be my new form of relaxation?
