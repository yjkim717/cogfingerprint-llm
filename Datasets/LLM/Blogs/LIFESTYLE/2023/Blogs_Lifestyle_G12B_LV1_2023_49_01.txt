## The Unexpected Comfort of Unraveling

It’s funny, isn’t it? How we cling to things, even when we know, deep down, they might not be serving us anymore. For years, my weekends were a predictable rhythm: Saturday morning farmers market, Sunday afternoon spent meticulously organizing my bookshelf. It was *my* thing, my little island of order in a chaotic world. A comforting routine, I told myself.

But lately, that comfort felt…stifling. Like a perfectly pressed shirt that’s just a bit too tight. This year, 2023, feels different. There’s a collective restlessness in the air, a feeling of needing to shift, to re-evaluate. And I think it’s seeped into my own life.

I realized I was resisting change, clinging to this routine because it felt safe, even though it was preventing me from exploring other things.  It felt like holding onto a political party you've always supported, even when you recognize the need for a new direction, a coalition of ideas.

So, I let it go.  I skipped the farmers market. I left the bookshelf delightfully disordered. And you know what? It wasn’t a disaster. It was…liberating.  I spent Saturday hiking, something I haven’t done in ages. Sunday?  I read a novel in a hammock, completely unburdened by the need to alphabetize.

It’s a small thing, but it's a reminder that sometimes, the greatest growth comes from accepting a shift, even – or especially – when it feels unsettling at first.  Perhaps embracing the "loss" of a cherished routine opens the door to unexpected and wonderful new chapters.
