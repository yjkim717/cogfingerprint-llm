## The Shiver Down Your Spine: When "Relaxing" Doesn't Feel So Relaxing

We’ve all heard it, right? Weed is *relaxing*. A chill way to unwind after a long day. And sometimes, it is. Truly. But lately, I’ve been thinking a lot about those times when it’s…not.

A friend recently shared a story – a brief, unsettling moment where things just felt *off*. A little blurry, a little distorted, a touch of something that made their skin crawl. They described it as a mild hallucination, and the sheer *unease* of it stuck with me.

It got me reflecting on my own experiences. There were times, particularly when I was younger and less mindful, where I'd chase that "perfect chill" and end up feeling…wired. Anxious. Like the world was subtly shifting beneath my feet. 

It's a reminder that what works for one person doesn’t always work for another. And that the expectation of a specific outcome – relaxation, creativity, whatever it may be – can actually create pressure that undermines the whole experience.  We're all navigating our mental landscapes, and sometimes, adding another element, even one that's perceived as 'safe' or ‘fun’, can throw things off balance.  It’s a good nudge to check in with yourself, and listen to what your body *actually* needs.
