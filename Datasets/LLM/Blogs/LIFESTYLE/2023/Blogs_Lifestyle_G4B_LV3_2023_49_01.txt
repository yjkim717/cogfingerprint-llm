**The Quiet After the Storm**

It’s funny, isn’t it? How easily we build up these grand expectations around things – especially elections. And when they don’t go the way we planned, there’s this immediate, almost visceral reaction. But lately, I’ve been thinking a lot about how we *manage* that quiet after the storm. 

This year, seeing the political landscape shift, it really made me consider the art of acceptance. Not just *accepting* the outcome, but actively shaping how we perceive it. It’s tempting to dwell on what *was*, to build a narrative of defeat. But honestly, isn’t it a more powerful strategy to frame it as a pause, a chance to regroup and build something new?  It’s about minimizing the negativity and recognizing that temporary setbacks can lead to bigger, brighter moments down the road.  What do you do to find that quiet strength after a challenge?
