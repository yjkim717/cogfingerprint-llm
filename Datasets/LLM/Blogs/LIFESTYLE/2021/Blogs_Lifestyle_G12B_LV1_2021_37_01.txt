## The Echo in the Room

It’s funny, isn’t it? How life can look… fine. Like, objectively, *fine*. I’m employed, I have a roof over my head, I’m (relatively) healthy. But lately, there’s this… echo. An emptiness that bounces around in the quiet moments, especially the long evenings.

I turned 31 this year, and I thought I'd feel *something* more profound. Like, a sense of established-ness. Instead, I'm battling this persistent feeling of detachment. It's not a crushing sadness, not exactly. It’s more like a low hum of… nothingness.

This year has been a bit of a rollercoaster. A breakup, navigating some serious debt (thanks, 2020!), and just generally feeling adrift. I've been trying things – new hobbies, reaching out to friends, even forcing myself to be “productive” – but it’s like I'm going through the motions. The motivation just isn't *there*.

I keep thinking about what truly matters. Not the things society tells me should matter – career advancement, a picture-perfect relationship – but what *actually* resonates with me. What makes me feel connected, engaged, like I’m contributing something worthwhile?

Honestly, I’m starting to think the answer isn't about *doing* more, but about *being* more present. About stripping away the noise and really listening to that quiet whisper within.  I’m exploring therapy, which feels like a big step, but a necessary one. Maybe finding meaningful work, something that feels aligned with my values, will help fill the echo.  It’s a journey, I know. But I’m hoping to quiet the emptiness, one small step at a time.
