**The Unexpected Debugging of My Life**

It’s funny, isn’t it? How you can sometimes find yourself staring at a problem that feels utterly baffling, and realize it’s not a technical glitch, but a fundamental misalignment. That’s how I felt a lot of 2021. I was chasing this feeling of “productivity,” meticulously scheduling every hour, trying to optimize my days like I was tweaking a 3D printer’s G-code. 

Then, I started wrestling with a frustrating issue on my Voron 2.4 – a persistent problem with the ERCF and purge commands during prints. Hours were spent analyzing the code, trying to isolate the error.  

It hit me eventually: I was so focused on *doing* everything efficiently, I’d forgotten to actually *enjoy* the process. Like a poorly executed print, my life was sputtering and failing.  Just like I needed to adjust the G-code, I needed to adjust my priorities.  Sometimes, the biggest breakthroughs come not from fixing a specific problem, but from stepping back and asking, "Am I really building something I want?"
