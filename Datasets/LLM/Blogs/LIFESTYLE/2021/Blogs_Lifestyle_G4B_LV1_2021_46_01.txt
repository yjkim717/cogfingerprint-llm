**The Quiet Hum of Obsession**

It’s funny, isn’t it? How a little project can take over. Last year, 2021, I was completely consumed by tweaking a Voron 2.4 3D printer – Klipper firmware, adxl345 sensors, the whole shebang. I was chasing pressure advance tuning, desperately trying to eliminate those little filament wiggles. It felt like resonance calibration was the key. 

There’s something oddly meditative about the process, a quiet hum of focused frustration.  Like trying to find the *perfect* speed for testing – was it too fast, too slow, just right?  It mirrored something I was grappling with in my own life – a need to optimize, to refine, to push for a feeling of…control. 

Ultimately, I realized the printer was just a distraction.  Maybe the real calibration was happening within myself.
