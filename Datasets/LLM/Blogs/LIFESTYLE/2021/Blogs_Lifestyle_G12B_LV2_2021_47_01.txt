**Title: The Gentle Art of Wrestling Time (and Winning… Sometimes)**

Remember 2021? It felt like a year where time both stretched and vanished in the blink of an eye. I was juggling work, trying to stay connected with friends (virtually, mostly!), and desperately attempting to learn sourdough bread baking. The result? A lot of good intentions and a whole lot of… well, *not* getting things done.

I realized I was a chronic procrastinator, a master of the “I’ll do it later” mentality. But "later" rarely arrived. So, I started experimenting. Simple things, really. Breaking down big tasks, actually *putting* things in my calendar (even fun stuff!), and, surprisingly, setting timers. 

It hasn't been a miraculous transformation. There are still days when the laundry piles up and the sourdough remains stubbornly flat. But consciously planning, scheduling, and giving myself focused bursts of work time? It’s made a real difference. It's about finding a rhythm, not achieving perfection. And honestly, that’s a lesson worth carrying forward.
