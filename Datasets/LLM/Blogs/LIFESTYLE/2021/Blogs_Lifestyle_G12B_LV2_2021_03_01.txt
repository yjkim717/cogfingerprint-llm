## The Weight of Things (and the Freedom of Letting Go)

It's late 2021, and I've officially committed to a solo thru-hike of the Continental Divide Trail next year. Sounds exciting, right? It is! But the planning… oh, the planning. Right now, it’s a deep dive into gear lists, obsessing over ounces, and wrestling with the realities of bear country. 

I’m aiming for “ultralight,” a concept that’s surprisingly heavy with expectation. It’s not just about the weight of my pack, though. It’s about the weight of *stuff* in general.  So much of our lives feels crammed with possessions, commitments, worries. 

This hike, weirdly, feels like a chance to shed some of that. To pare things down – not just my gear, but the mental baggage too.  A bear canister feels like a good metaphor for that, actually: a contained space for essentials, leaving room to breathe. Maybe simplifying my life, even just for a few months, is the biggest adventure of all.
