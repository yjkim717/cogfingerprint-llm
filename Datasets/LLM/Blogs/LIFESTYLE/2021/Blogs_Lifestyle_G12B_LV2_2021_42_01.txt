**Title: The Bread, The Milk, and All Our Worries**

Remember that snowstorm we were supposed to get last week? The one that ended up being, like, three fluffy inches? I do. Because I spent it working a shift at the grocery store watching a full-blown *panic* unfold. Shelves stripped bare of bread and milk. Seriously, bread and milk!

It struck me as I was restocking the canned goods – this isn’t just about snow. It’s about *something* deeper. 2021 has been… a lot. We’re all a little frayed around the edges, aren't we? And when a tiny trigger – a weather forecast, a news headline – pops up, it feels like the floodgates open.

I get it.  Wanting to feel prepared is natural. But seeing grown adults practically wrestling over the last loaf of sourdough felt… sad. Like a collective exhale of anxiety, manifested in empty shelves. Maybe we need to remember to breathe a little deeper, and trust that a little snow – or whatever life throws our way – isn’t the end of the world. We’ll figure it out. We always do.
