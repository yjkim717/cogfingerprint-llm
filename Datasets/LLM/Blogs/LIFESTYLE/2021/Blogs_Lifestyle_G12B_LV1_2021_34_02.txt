## The Unexpected Stalling

It's funny, isn't it, how life sometimes feels like a really old diesel engine? You’re cruising along, feeling like you've got momentum, then suddenly…nothing. A stutter. A hesitation. A complete loss of throttle response.

That’s pretty much how the last few months have felt. 2021 started with such promise. I’d finally carved out a routine I was happy with, a little garden thriving, a decent work-life balance… I felt like I was really *moving*. And then, seemingly out of nowhere, things started to stall.

It wasn't a catastrophic breakdown, more like a creeping sense of disconnect. Suddenly, the things I usually found joy in felt…flat. Motivation evaporated. Simple tasks felt monumental. It was like the engine was trying to fire up, the glow plugs were *engaged*, but the whole system just wasn't cooperating.

I started to wonder if it was something fundamental, like a faulty sensor – the metaphorical ICP, if you will. Was there a deeper issue that needed addressing?  The PCM, the central control unit of my life, felt overloaded, struggling to process everything.

It’s been a process of troubleshooting, really.  Taking a step back, examining the "wiring" of my days, trying to identify the points of resistance.  It's not a quick fix, and I suspect it won't be. But acknowledging the stalling, identifying the possible culprits, feels like the first step towards getting things running smoothly again.  Maybe a little recalibration is all I need.  And maybe, just maybe, a little patience with the process.  Because even the most reliable engines need a tune-up now and then.
