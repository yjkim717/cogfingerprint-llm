"Snow Panic: A Reflection on Our Collective Anxiety"

As I scrolled through the news in 2021, I came across a retail worker's rant about the chaos caused by a predicted light snowfall. The images of empty shelves and long lines stuck with me. It wasn't just the snow that triggered the panic buying, but our collective anxiety. We'd been conditioned to overreact to the slightest disruption. The worker's frustration resonated with me - I'd been there too, waiting in line for essentials that were suddenly in short supply. It made me reflect on how we respond to uncertainty. Do we let fear dictate our actions, or do we take a step back and assess the situation? As we navigate the uncertainties of life, let's take a moment to breathe and ask ourselves: are we reacting, or are we responding?
