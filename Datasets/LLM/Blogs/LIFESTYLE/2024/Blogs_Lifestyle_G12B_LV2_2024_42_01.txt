## The Invisible Line & Why We All Need a Little Space

Isn't it wild how easily a seemingly small thing can throw off your entire mood? Lately, it’s been tailgaters for me. Not the ones legitimately trying to merge or when traffic's at a standstill – I get that. But the ones just… *there*, hovering, radiating impatience when I’m already going the speed limit. 

It’s more than just annoying; it's unsettling. It makes me think about all the invisible lines we draw in life – the personal space we crave, the emotional distance we need. We’re all rushing, stressed, juggling a million things, but that doesn't give anyone the right to make someone else feel unsafe or pressured.

Maybe it’s a sign I need to build a little more buffer into *everything*. A few extra minutes in the morning, a little more quiet time in the evening. Creating that space, both on the road and in life, feels like a small act of self-care these days. What’s your “pet peeve” that actually reveals a deeper need?
