As I bent down to prune the roses, I felt a sudden wave of dizziness wash over me. I stood up, thinking it was just a minor moment of lightheadedness, but it only got worse. A metallic taste filled my mouth, and a throbbing headache began to build. I knew I had to get out of the sun, pronto!

As a 40-year-old woman, I've always been relatively healthy, so I was taken aback by my body's sudden reaction. I realized that I had been neglecting my own health while tending to my garden. The heat, dehydration, and physical exertion had all taken their toll.

This alarming experience was a wake-up call. I sought medical attention, and thankfully, it was just a case of heat exhaustion.
