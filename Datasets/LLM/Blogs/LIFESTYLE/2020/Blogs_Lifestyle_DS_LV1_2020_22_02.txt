The basement had become our pandemic sanctuary—my makeshift office, the kids’ play zone. But when the radon detector’s reading crept into the red zone, that safe space suddenly felt anything but.

I wrestled with it for days: invite a mitigation crew into our home amid COVID fears, or ignore the invisible threat lurking beneath us? In 2020, every choice felt weighted with risk.

Ultimately, we chose long-term health over short-term comfort. Sometimes safety means confronting what we can’t see—whether it’s a virus or radioactive gas—and making the hard calls for those we love.
