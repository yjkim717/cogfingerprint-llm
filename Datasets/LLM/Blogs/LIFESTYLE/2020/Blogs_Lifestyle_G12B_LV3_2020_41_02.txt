## The Illusion of "Customization" (and That Liberty Mutual Commercial)

Remember back in 2020, when everything felt…uncertain? I was scrolling Reddit the other day and stumbled upon someone brilliantly dissecting a Liberty Mutual car insurance commercial. It hit me – how much of what we’re sold is *actually* customized? We crave personalization, but often end up with slight variations of the same thing. It’s a funny, slightly unsettling reflection on consumerism, isn't it?
