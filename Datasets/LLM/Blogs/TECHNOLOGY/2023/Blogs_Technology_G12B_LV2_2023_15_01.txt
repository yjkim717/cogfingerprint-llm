## The Ghostly Glow of a Screen-Lit Night

It's funny, isn't it, how reliant we've become on these little rectangles? I'm lying in bed now, scrolling, as I often am. It’s late, the house is quiet, and the blue light from my phone is painting the ceiling a strange, ethereal blue. I remember a time when bedtime meant a book, a warm drink, and… darkness. Real, total darkness.

Now, it's a constant battle against the digital glow. We’re told to dim the screens, use blue light filters, but the pull is strong. It’s a comfort, a connection, a rabbit hole of endless information.

I recently realized how much this constant light exposure might be impacting my sleep. I miss the deep, restorative kind – the kind that comes with a truly dark room. It’s a small thing, maybe, but it’s made me think about all the subtle ways technology, meant to enhance our lives, can also quietly disrupt them. It’s a trade-off, isn't it? A constant balancing act. Maybe tomorrow, I’ll put the phone down a little earlier.
