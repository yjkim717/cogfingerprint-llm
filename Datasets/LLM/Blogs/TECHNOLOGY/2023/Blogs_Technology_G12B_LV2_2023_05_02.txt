## The Quiet Hum of Always-On

It’s funny, isn’t it? How quickly “revolutionary” tech just…becomes the wallpaper of our lives. Remember when smartphones felt like a futuristic leap? Now, I barely notice I’m holding one until I need to Google something obscure (usually related to a recipe I'm failing at).

This year, especially, I’ve been thinking about this constant connection. It’s amazing we can video call family across the globe, or learn a new skill with a few taps. But there's also a quiet hum of *always-on* that can be a bit exhausting. 

I’ve been consciously trying to carve out little pockets of digital silence - a walk without headphones, reading a physical book, just *being* without the urge to document it. It’s a small thing, but it feels… restorative. Maybe the next revolution isn't about *more* technology, but about learning to disconnect from it, intentionally. What do you think?
