## The Disappearing Art of Just *Being*

Do you ever feel it? That low hum of anxiety, the constant pull to check your phone, even when you know there's absolutely nothing new? I do. A lot. 2023 has been… intense. And technology, while connecting us in incredible ways, also feels like it's subtly stealing something precious: the ability to just *be*.

I caught myself the other day, sitting on my porch, actually enjoying the quiet of a summer evening. Then, almost instinctively, I reached for my phone. No reason. Just… habit. It was a jarring moment. 

We're so busy documenting experiences, curating online personas, and reacting to the endless stream of information that we forget to just *feel* them. To savor the stillness. To notice the way the light filters through the trees.

I’m not advocating for ditching tech entirely (hello, how else would I be writing this?!), but I think it's worth consciously carving out moments to disconnect. To reclaim those pockets of quiet where thoughts can breathe, and life can simply… unfold. What do you think? Are you feeling the pull too?
