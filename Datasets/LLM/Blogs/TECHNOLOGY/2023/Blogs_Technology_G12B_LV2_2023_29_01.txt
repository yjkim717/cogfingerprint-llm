**Title: The Echo Chamber and the Algorithm of Experience**

It's 2023, and the internet is *full* of advice. Especially in tech. We’re constantly bombarded with “best practices,” “must-learn skills,” and pronouncements from industry leaders. Lately, I’ve been thinking a lot about how much of it we should actually *take* to heart.

I stumbled across a really insightful piece the other day about language design, and it hit me: even seasoned language architects admit that what worked for them – a particular design choice, a specific tooling approach – might be completely wrong for someone else’s project, their team, or even their own future selves.

It’s a humbling thought. We all get caught up in the echo chamber, admiring the solutions of those we perceive as successful. But their journey, their constraints, their *experience* are uniquely theirs.  Maybe the best advice isn't to *follow*, but to deeply understand *why* someone made a decision, and then thoughtfully apply (or reject!) it to your own context.  What do *you* think?
