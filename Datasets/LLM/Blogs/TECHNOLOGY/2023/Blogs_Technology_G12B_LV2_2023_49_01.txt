**Title: The Ghosts in My Photo Roll**

It's funny, isn’t it? We live in an age where capturing a moment is so ridiculously easy. A tap, a click, and *bam* – a perfect snapshot, instantly backed up to the cloud. I’ve got terabytes of photos and videos on my phone, a digital time capsule of… well, everything.

But lately, scrolling through it feels less like reminiscing and more like encountering ghosts.  Not spooky ones, but the gentle, melancholic echoes of lives lived.  There’s my niece’s fifth birthday party, captured in shaky video. My dad’s goofy grin from last Christmas.  Friends I haven't spoken to in months, frozen in a sunny park scene.

I used to rely on memory, on the imperfect, fading details of experiences. Now, it feels like I outsource it to my phone. Do I *remember* that birthday party, or do I just remember the *video* of it?

It’s not about deleting everything, of course. But it’s a quiet nudge to be *present* more. To actually *feel* the moment, instead of just framing it for posterity. Maybe the best memories aren't the ones perfectly preserved in pixels, but the ones that live, slightly blurry and incomplete, in our hearts.
