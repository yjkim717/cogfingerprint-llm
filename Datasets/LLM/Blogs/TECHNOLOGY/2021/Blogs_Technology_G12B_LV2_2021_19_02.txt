## The Algorithm Knows... But Does *I*?

It's funny, isn’t it? We’re constantly told about the power of algorithms. They curate our news feeds, recommend our next binge-watch, even decide what ads we see. In 2021, it feels like life is increasingly filtered, predicted, and suggested by lines of code.

Recently, I was wrestling with a surprisingly similar feeling, but in a totally different context. I was helping a friend with D&D, and they were getting tripped up on how many spells their Bard could *know* versus *prepare*. It felt… oddly relatable.

We spend so much time optimizing our digital lives – streamlining workflows, using productivity apps, chasing efficiency.  But sometimes I wonder if we're outsourcing our decision-making too much. Are we so reliant on recommendations and data that we forget to actually *think* for ourselves?

The Bard, at least, had a clear rulebook. Algorithms? Not always so transparent. Maybe the key isn't to fight the tech, but to be more mindful of *why* we're accepting its suggestions. To remember that we, like a well-prepared Bard, still need to choose our path.
