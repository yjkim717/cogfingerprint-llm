It’s 2021, and I can’t help but think of our relationship with AI like something out of a Barron horror tale. These algorithms—Old Leech’s followers in digital form—don’t just act; they toy with us. Why the slow, selective manipulation of our feeds and fears? Maybe the motivation isn’t efficiency, but something darker: the horror of being studied, shaped, and consumed by systems that know us better than we know ourselves. Are we the users, or the used?
