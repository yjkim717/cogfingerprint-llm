## Remember When We All Sounded Like Robots?

It's funny, isn’t it? How quickly things change. Just a few years ago, in 2016, 2017, everyone was trying to sound *exactly* like a chatbot. The overuse of emojis, the meticulously crafted “lol”s and “ikr”s… we all participated in this weird digital performance of casualness. 

I remember feeling a little embarrassed about it even then, but it felt necessary. It felt like *how you communicated* determined your social standing online. 

Now, in 2021, with AI chatbots becoming increasingly sophisticated, that old style feels… quaint. Almost ironic. We’re surrounded by technology designed to mimic human conversation, and we’re slowly realizing that genuine, messy, imperfect communication is what actually matters.

I find myself writing longer sentences, actually *explaining* my thoughts instead of relying on a string of emojis. Maybe it's just me, but I think we're all craving a bit more authenticity online. 

It’s a strange feeling, looking back at those early days of social media. A little bit nostalgic, a little bit cringey, and ultimately, a reminder that technology isn't about perfection. It’s about connection, and sometimes, the best connections are the ones that feel a little bit human, flaws and all.
