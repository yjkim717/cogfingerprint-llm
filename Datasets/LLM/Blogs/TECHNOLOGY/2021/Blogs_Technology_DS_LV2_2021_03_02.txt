Of course, here is a reflective blog post from 2021.

Remember the frantic scramble for a next-gen console in late 2020? I finally snagged a PlayStation 5 in early 2021, expecting a graphical leap. But the real surprise was the controller.

The first time I felt its adaptive triggers resist in a shooter, or the subtle haptic feedback mimic rain in a puzzle game, it wasn't just a new feature—it was a reconnection. In a year of digital overload and endless Zooms, this small piece of technology managed to bridge the gap between my screen and my senses. It was a tactile reminder of the pure, physical joy of play that I’d missed. Sometimes, the biggest technological leaps aren't in what we see, but in what we feel.
