## The Algorithm Knows Me Better Than I Know Myself (And That's Kind of Creepy)

It’s 2021, and I’m officially convinced the internet knows me better than I know myself. Like, *really* knows me. Remember when Facebook used to just show you ads for things you'd vaguely mentioned in passing? That feels like ancient history now.

Lately, it's been… unsettling. The suggested articles, the videos popping up, they’re hitting a little *too* close to home. It's not just about shopping anymore. It's about reinforcing existing beliefs, feeding into anxieties I didn’t even realize I had.

I find myself questioning things more. Not just about what I'm seeing online, but about *everything*. We're constantly bombarded with information, curated experiences, and echo chambers that just amplify what we already think. It's easy to get lost in it all, to lose track of what's real and what's… optimized.

It’s not that I’m suddenly a Luddite, wanting to chuck my phone into the sea. Technology is incredible. But this constant feeling of being *seen* – analyzed, categorized, and predicted – it's a strange sensation. 

I wonder if anyone else feels this way? Like we’re all participating in a giant, slightly unsettling experiment, where the algorithm is the scientist and we’re… well, we’re the data.  Maybe it's time we all take a step back and consciously choose what we consume, and *why*. Before the algorithm chooses for us.
