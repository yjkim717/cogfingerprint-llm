Okay, so I've been feeling…off. Like a computer running too many programs at once. You know that sluggish feeling, where everything takes longer, and you're constantly battling a low-level headache? Yeah, that's been my reality lately.

It's weird, because I *thought* I was handling it all. Juggling freelance writing, keeping up with the endless stream of TikToks (research, obviously!), and trying to maintain some semblance of a social life. 2022, right? Peak digital overwhelm.

But I started noticing these little things. A persistent fatigue that no amount of sleep seemed to shake. Random tingling in my fingers when I'd spend too long hunched over my laptop. And the headaches? They were escalating. 

It got me thinking – is it possible to overload *yourself* with information, with constant connection? Is it possible to, in a weird way, create a kind of…digital pressure? I know it sounds dramatic, but it feels like my brain is struggling to process it all. 

I’m not suggesting we all unplug entirely (though, maybe a digital detox *is* in order). But I think we need to be more mindful of how much we're loading onto our mental circuits. Maybe it’s about setting boundaries, saying "no" more often, and actively creating space for…well, just *being*.
