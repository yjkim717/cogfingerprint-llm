>## The Little Things That Make You Question Everything

It’s 2022, and we’re surrounded by incredible tech. Phones that fit in our palms, cars that practically drive themselves… yet, I spent a solid hour today wrestling with a tiny, stubborn screw on my beloved espresso machine. A *single* screw. 

It felt like a metaphor, honestly. We chase the groundbreaking, the revolutionary, and sometimes overlook the foundational stuff. This little metal piece, crucial for the whole operation, crumbled under pressure. It made me wonder: are we so focused on the big leaps that we’re neglecting the reliability of the smaller components? Anyone else feel this way?
