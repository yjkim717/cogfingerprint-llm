## The App Graveyard & Finding My Focus Again

Remember when every productivity app promised to *revolutionize* your life? 2023 was peak app-download frenzy for me. Notion, Todoist, Obsidian… my phone felt less like a communication tool and more like a digital command center. And honestly? It was exhausting. 

I found myself spending more time *organizing* my tasks than actually *doing* them. My digital desk became a chaotic landscape of half-finished projects and abandoned templates. It felt… counterproductive.

This year, I've been quietly dismantling that system. Deleting apps. Turning off notifications. Embracing the slightly terrifying simplicity of a paper notebook. It’s been surprisingly liberating.  There's a quiet joy in the deliberate act of writing things down, a tangible connection that pixels just can’t replicate.  It’s not about rejecting technology entirely, but about reclaiming space – mental and digital – for what truly matters.  Anyone else feel like they’re decluttering their digital lives in 2024?
