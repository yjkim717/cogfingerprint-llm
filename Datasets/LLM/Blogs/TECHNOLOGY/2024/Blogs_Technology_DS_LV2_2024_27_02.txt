It’s 2024, and my phone now predicts my coffee order before I do. This “smart” life feels a lot like that overpowered Checkered Deck in Balatro—flawless, consistent, almost too easy. But where’s the challenge, the delightful friction of a forgotten password or a wrong turn that leads somewhere new? I’m starting to miss the messy, human unpredictability. Perfect consistency is impressive, but it’s the stumbles that often make the better story.
