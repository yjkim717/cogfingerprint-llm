## Does Anyone Else Feel… a Little Overwhelmed by "Smart"?

It's 2024, and everything is *smart*. My fridge practically judges my grocery choices, my watch monitors my sleep (or lack thereof), and I swear my phone knows what I'm going to type before *I* do. It’s incredible, really. We've built this world of seamless convenience.

But lately, I've been feeling… a little weary. Like, are we sacrificing something in the name of efficiency? I find myself craving moments of blissful disconnect, a time when my brain isn't buzzing with notifications or optimized routines. 

Remember when a map was a folded piece of paper? Or when remembering birthdays was a *thing* you actually had to do? There’s a quiet charm to those little imperfections, a human element that feels increasingly rare. Maybe "smart" isn't always better. Maybe sometimes, a little bit of analog is exactly what we need. What do you think?
