It’s 2020, and I’m knee-deep in cables again—VGA to CRT, RetroArch humming on an old laptop. Everyone’s chasing 4K, but here I am, chasing ghosts. The scanlines flicker to life, and suddenly, I’m 10 years old in my parents’ basement. It’s not just nostalgia; it’s the warmth, the slight blur, the way pixels breathe. In a world obsessed with perfection, sometimes the magic is in the flaws. Who knew an old TV could feel so revolutionary?
