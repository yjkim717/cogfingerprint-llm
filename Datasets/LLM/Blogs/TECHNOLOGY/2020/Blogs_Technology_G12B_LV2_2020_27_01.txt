## The Quiet Hum of Disconnection

Remember early 2020? The world felt…loud. Not in a bad way, initially. Everyone was connecting, sharing, learning, *everything* was online. I was swept up in it too, refreshing news feeds, joining countless video calls, trying to absorb all the information and maintain every connection. It was exhilarating, but quickly exhausting.

I found myself scrolling endlessly, feeling less connected than ever. The constant stream of updates, opinions, and anxieties started to feel like a weight. So, I did something slightly radical: I started scheduling digital breaks. Actual, intentional moments of *nothing*.

Just sitting. Listening to the rain. Reading a physical book. It sounds simple, maybe even a little silly, but those quiet moments, away from the hum of notifications, have been surprisingly restorative. It’s a reminder that true connection isn't about being *always* online, but about being present, wherever you are. And sometimes, the most meaningful connection is with yourself.
