Back in 2020, as I trimmed my PhD statement of purpose from 1500 to 1000 words, I felt like I was compressing my entire academic passion into a tiny digital box. Each cut stung, fearing I’d lose the essence. But that editing process taught me a vital tech-era lesson: clarity often emerges from constraints. Just as algorithms refine data, thoughtful revision sharpens purpose. Sometimes, less code—or fewer words—truly creates a more powerful impact.
