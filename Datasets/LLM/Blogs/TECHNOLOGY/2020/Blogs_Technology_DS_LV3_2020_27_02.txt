There’s a heaviness that’s settled in this year, a low hum of anger I can’t seem to mute. I’ve tried logging off—deleting Twitter, avoiding the news feeds. But the tension from COVID and racial injustice seeps in anyway, coloring everything. My phone feels like a conduit for the world’s frustration, a constant reminder of the divides. Even in the quiet of my Southern town, the digital echo of 2020 makes finding peace a daily, deliberate battle.
