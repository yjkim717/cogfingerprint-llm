## The Unexpected Loneliness of My Laptop

Remember March 2020? The world shifted on a dime. Suddenly, my tiny apartment became my office, my gym, my social hub… everything. Like so many others, I embraced the “work from home” revolution. Initially, it was amazing! No commute, more time for coffee, the freedom to wear pajamas all day. Productivity, I thought, would skyrocket.

And in some ways, it did. But something else crept in – a quiet loneliness.

I used to thrive on the casual banter around the office coffee machine, the spontaneous brainstorming sessions, even the slightly irritating office chatter. Those seemingly insignificant interactions were, I realized, vital. They were the threads that wove a sense of community. Now, my days were punctuated by the click of keys and the glow of my laptop screen.

Video calls helped, of course, but they weren't the same. There was a distance, a performative quality to them.  It felt like we were all trying a little too hard to seem engaged.

It’s been a year now, and while I appreciate the flexibility, I’m acutely aware of the human cost of this digital shift.  Technology promised connection, but sometimes, it just amplifies the quiet spaces between us. I’m learning to be more intentional about reaching out, about creating those connections, even if it’s just a quick phone call to a friend. It’s a small thing, but it makes a difference.
