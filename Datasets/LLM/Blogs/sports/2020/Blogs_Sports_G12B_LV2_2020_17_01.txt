**Title: The Empty Bleachers and a Lesson in Letting Go**

Remember 2020? It feels like a lifetime ago, doesn't it? For me, a huge chunk of that year revolved around my son’s soccer. He was eight, obsessed with the beautiful game, and I was… well, I was the team’s loudest cheerleader, rain or shine. Every Saturday morning, we’d be there, bundled up, yelling encouragement. It was *our* thing.

Then, everything changed. The pandemic hit, games were cancelled, and suddenly, the field was eerily empty. It wasn't just the soccer we missed; it was the community, the routine, the shared joy.

It forced me to confront something I hadn't realized – how much of my identity had become wrapped up in being "the soccer mom." It was a tough lesson in letting go, in realizing that his passion, his journey, was ultimately *his*. And that my role wasn't to be a constant presence on the sidelines, but to support him from wherever he needed. It was a strange, quiet year, but it taught me a lot about perspective and the beauty of watching from a distance.
