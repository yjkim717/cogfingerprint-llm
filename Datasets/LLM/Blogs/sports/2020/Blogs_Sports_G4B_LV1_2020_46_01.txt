**The Unexpected Geometry of Lockdown**

It feels like a lifetime ago, doesn’t it? 2020. Everything felt…still. I was stuck at home, staring at my pegboard more than I cared to admit. I’d always dabbled in Perler beads, little mosaics mostly. But then, I started experimenting with building *bigger* – almost ridiculously large designs. 

It was oddly meditative, like adding tiny, colorful pieces to a puzzle. I’d lay down a base, add a few strategically placed beads, then peel them off and rearrange. It felt a bit like rebuilding a game plan after a frustrating loss.  Each adjustment, each shift in color, was a small, deliberate attempt to improve. 

Maybe that's what lockdown taught me – that sometimes, the best way forward is to dismantle and rebuild, piece by piece.
