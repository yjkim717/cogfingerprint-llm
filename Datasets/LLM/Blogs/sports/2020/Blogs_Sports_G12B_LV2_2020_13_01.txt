**The Ghost of the '06 Red Sox and a Late-Night Text**

It’s weird, isn’t it, how certain moments in sports just…stick? Like a burr on your sock. For me, it’s the 2006 American League Championship Series. Red Sox versus the Detroit Tigers. Game 5. Mark Little’s bloop single. The collective exhale of Boston. The feeling that *this* was finally it.

2020 has been…a lot. We all know that. A weird, isolating kind of year. And honestly, I've found myself retreating into memories, clinging to the familiar comfort of past glories. I was scrolling through baseball stats the other night – purely for nostalgia, you understand – when I got a random text.

It was from a number I didn't recognize. Just a simple, “Still remember that Little single, huh?”

My first thought was, “Who *is* this? And how did they get my number?” A little bit of that 2020 paranoia crept in. But then…I paused. It was just someone else who remembered. Someone else who felt that same jolt of electricity, that shared history of a moment in time.

We exchanged a few messages. Turns out, he's a lifelong Tigers fan, living in Michigan. We argued playfully about the game, about baseball in general. It was a fleeting connection, sparked by something completely unexpected, something that felt oddly... comforting in a year that desperately needed it. 

It reminded me that even across miles, across rivalries, we’re all bound together by these shared passions. And sometimes, a simple text from a stranger can be a surprisingly powerful reminder of that.
