## The Empty Stands and the Unexpected Lesson

2020. Just saying the year feels… heavy, doesn't it? We all remember it. The world felt like it held its breath for a while. For me, a huge part of that feeling was the silence surrounding sports. I’m a lifelong baseball fan, and watching games played in those eerie, empty stadiums felt fundamentally *wrong*. No roar of the crowd, no shared anticipation, just… stillness.

I found myself strangely missing the frustration of a dropped ball, the collective groan of disappointment. It made me realize how much of the experience was about *us*, the fans, as much as it was about the players.

It wasn’t just baseball, either. Every sport felt muted. But in that quiet, I started to appreciate the dedication of the athletes themselves. They were competing, pushing themselves, even without the usual energy feeding off the crowd. It was a lesson in perseverance, in finding motivation from within, that I think we all needed in 2020. And honestly? It's a lesson I’ve carried with me since. Sometimes, the greatest triumphs come in the quietest moments.
