**The Silent Bench**

It’s weird, isn’t it? How a single moment can stick with you, long after it’s happened. Back in 2020, I was really locked into watching the Lakers, glued to the screen during that weird, isolated season. I was part of a pretty vocal online group – a subreddit, really – dissecting every play, every decision. 

I got involved in a particularly nasty exchange about LeBron’s passing. It started as a critique, but quickly devolved into a shouting match, fueled by frustration and, honestly, a bit of ego. I said some things I instantly regretted.  

The next morning, I woke up to find myself banned. Permanently. It felt… brutal. Like being exiled from a favorite bench.  I realized I’d let a digital argument overshadow the actual game, the shared love of the sport. It was a harsh reminder that online spaces can easily amplify negativity.  It’s a lesson I still carry with me – sometimes, the best thing to do is just step away and enjoy the view.
