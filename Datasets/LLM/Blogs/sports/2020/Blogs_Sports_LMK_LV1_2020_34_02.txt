The 2020 college football season was shaping up to be a wild ride, especially for SEC teams. As they navigated the scheduling landscape, a pressing question emerged: how to balance nine conference games with attractive non-conference matchups. With ACC titans on the horizon, the stakes were high. Coaches and athletic directors were tasked with crafting a schedule that would satisfy both the SEC's rigorous conference slate and the allure of high-profile non-conference games. It was a delicate dance, but one that promised to yield exciting matchups and unforgettable moments.
