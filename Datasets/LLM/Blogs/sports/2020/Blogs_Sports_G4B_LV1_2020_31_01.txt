**The Year the Stadiums Echoed with Absence**

Honestly, 2020 felt… strange. We were all trying to figure out how to live in a world where the biggest thrill was ordering takeout and watching a screen. And for a sports fan like me, it was a particularly tough year. Remember the sheer *anticipation* leading up to the NBA bubble? It felt like the biggest event on the planet, and then… it was just a bubble. 

I missed the roar of the crowd, the electric atmosphere, the feeling of being *there*. I watched my local rugby team’s games on a tiny laptop screen, feeling miles away and completely disconnected. It wasn’t the same. 

It wasn’t just the professional leagues, either. High school football games, little league baseball – all gone. The sports I grew up with, suddenly inaccessible.  

There were bright spots, of course. The outpouring of support for the NHS, the incredible acts of kindness we saw during lockdown. But looking back, 2020 was defined by a profound sense of loss.  It made me realize how much we take for granted – the simple joy of being in a stadium, surrounded by fellow fans. 

It’s been a long year, and while things are slowly getting back to normal, a little piece of that sporting excitement feels like it’s still missing.
