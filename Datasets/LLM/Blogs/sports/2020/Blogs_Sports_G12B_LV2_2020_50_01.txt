## The Missed Call and the Echo of the Year

Remember 2020? Feels like a lifetime ago, doesn't it? For me, it wasn't just the pandemic that blurred the edges of time. It was the sudden, strange quiet. No roaring crowds, no packed stadiums, just… stillness. I found myself hyper-focused on the little things, the details I'd always overlooked. 

Like phone calls. Specifically, missing them. My dad, a lifelong Cardinals fan, started leaving me voicemails about every game. Bless his heart, he'd get so excited, recounting every play, but sometimes… the connection was rough. I’d have to call him back, asking him to repeat himself, feeling a little frustrated. 

It struck me then: sometimes, even when we're trying to share something important, something we're passionate about, clarity matters. Slowing down, making sure the message gets through. 2020 taught me a lot about appreciating connection, and sometimes, that means just taking a breath and speaking clearly.  It's a lesson that extends far beyond baseball scores.
