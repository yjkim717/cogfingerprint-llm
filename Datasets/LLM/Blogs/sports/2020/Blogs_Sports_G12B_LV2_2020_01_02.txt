## The Quiet of the Empty Stands

2020. A year that redefined "unprecedented," right? For me, a huge part of that strangeness was the silence surrounding my usual baseball obsession. Normally, April means packed stands, the crack of the bat, the roar of the crowd – a vibrant energy. But this year? Empty stadiums. Games on TV felt…hollow.

It made me realize how much I *missed* the communal experience. It wasn’t just about the game itself; it was the shared excitement with strangers, the collective gasp at a close play, the feeling of belonging.  Suddenly, watching alone felt less like a sport and more like a solitary exercise.  It was a stark reminder that sometimes, the best parts of life aren't about the individual achievement, but the shared moments we experience *together*. Hoping for a full crowd again soon.
