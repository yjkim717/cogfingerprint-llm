## The Bubble and the Echoes

2020. Just saying the year feels heavy, doesn't it? And sports… sports were *something else* that year. I remember feeling so bizarrely invested in the NBA bubble in Orlando. Like, here we were, a world grappling with a pandemic, and I was glued to my screen, willing the Lakers to pull it together. 

It felt so surreal. Empty arenas, players wearing masks when they weren’t actively competing, the whole thing unfolding in this carefully constructed environment. It was a weird escape, I guess. A little pocket of normalcy, or at least *something* familiar, in a year that was anything but.

I kept thinking about how much it reflected the rest of our lives at the time – isolated, trying to create order within controlled parameters. And then, the echoes. Even now, years later, I get a pang of that 2020 feeling when I see a sparsely populated stadium. It's a reminder of a time when the world felt… different. A time when even cheering for a team felt laced with a quiet melancholy.
