It’s 2020, and the world feels upside down. My daily run became my anchor—the one thing I could control when everything else was chaos. Lacing up my worn-out sneakers, I’d hit the pavement, each step a small rebellion against uncertainty. Some days were sluggish, others surprisingly swift, but every mile taught me resilience. In a year of distance, running reminded me that forward motion, however slow, is still progress. And sometimes, that’s enough.
