## The Unexpected Lesson from a Lost Soccer Game

It's funny how life throws curveballs, isn’t it? My son, Leo, was *obsessed* with soccer this year. 2021 felt… chaotic, to say the least, and watching him run around, kicking that ball with such joyful abandon, was a little slice of normalcy. He’s six, and this was his first real season with a team.

We went into the final game needing a win to make the playoffs. Leo was so excited, bouncing off the walls. But… we lost. Badly. There were tears, mostly from him. I wanted to scoop him up, tell him it didn't matter, but I held back.

Instead, I just sat beside him on the bench and watched him process it. And you know what? He started talking. About how the other team was fast, about a missed goal, about wanting to practice harder. It wasn't about the loss itself, but about the *effort* and the learning.

That’s when it hit me. Sometimes, the biggest wins aren’t on the scoreboard. Sometimes, they're in the quiet moments of resilience, the lessons learned from disappointment. And honestly, that’s a far more valuable game to be playing.
