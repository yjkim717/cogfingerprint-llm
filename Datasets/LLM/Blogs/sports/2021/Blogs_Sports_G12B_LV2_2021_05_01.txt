## The Unexpected Joy of Starting Over (and My Terrible Pickleball)

Okay, so I've officially accepted defeat. My pickleball game is… not good. Like, *really* not good. My friend, Liam, has been patiently trying to teach me for weeks, and I’m still mostly just flailing and apologizing for hitting the ball into the fence.

It’s funny, isn't it? As adults, we get so caught up in being "good" at things. We’ve spent years building careers, honing skills, figuring out our routines. The idea of being a beginner again feels… uncomfortable.

But something about this pickleball struggle has been surprisingly refreshing.  It’s stripped away the pressure of performance and just left me with the pure joy (and occasional frustration!) of learning. I’m not trying to be a pro; I’m just trying to *hit the ball over the net*.  

It reminded me of something my dad used to say: "Even the best players are always learning." And he's right. Watching the US Open this year, seeing those incredible athletes constantly adjusting their strategies, adapting to their opponents... it’s proof that growth is a never-ending process.

Maybe it’s 2021, and we all need a little reminder to embrace the awkwardness of being a beginner. To find something new to fumble through, to rediscover the simple pleasure of trying, even if you’re terrible at it.  I’m going to keep hitting that pickleball, even if it mostly ends up in the neighbor’s yard. At least I'm having fun.
