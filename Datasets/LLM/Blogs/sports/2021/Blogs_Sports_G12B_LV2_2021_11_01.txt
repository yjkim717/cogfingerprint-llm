## The Quiet Joy of Watching Someone Else Shine

Remember that feeling? It’s late summer, 2021, and the Olympics were in full swing. Everyone was buzzing about medal counts and viral moments, but I found myself unexpectedly moved by something quieter. It wasn’t the gold, it was the *effort*.

I was watching a longshot swimmer, someone who hadn't qualified for a major international meet before. They weren't expected to win, not even close. But they swam their heart out. Every stroke, every breath, visible determination etched on their face. And honestly? That was more inspiring than any record-breaking performance.

It made me think about all the things we pursue, the goals we set, and the pressure we put on ourselves. Sometimes, it’s okay to just *try*. To put in the work, to push yourself, even if the outcome isn't what you envisioned. There's a genuine beauty in that dedication, in witnessing someone give it their absolute all. It’s a reminder that the journey, the striving, can be just as rewarding as the destination. And sometimes, just sometimes, it’s enough to watch someone else experience that joy.
