## The Quiet Sundays I Missed

It’s funny the things you realize you miss when they’re…gone, or at least different. 2021 has been a year of shifts, hasn't it? For me, one of the small, unexpected things I've been thinking about is Sunday afternoon football with my dad and brother.

We used to have this ritual. Every Sunday, no matter what, we'd gather around the TV, surrounded by the smell of my mom's chili. Arguments were inevitable – usually about questionable calls – but there was this comforting familiarity to it all. My dad, a lifelong [Insert favorite team here - e.g., Packers] fan, would analyze every play with a seriousness that was both endearing and slightly ridiculous. My brother and I would mostly just eat the chili and heckle him.

This year, things changed. My brother moved across the country, and Dad… well, Dad's been busy with his own things. The Sunday routines faded. Now, I catch games in snippets, alone. It’s convenient, sure, but it lacks that…something. 

It’s not just about the football. It's about the time spent, the shared history, the comfortable silence between the shouts.  It's a reminder that even the most familiar routines can shift, and sometimes, what you miss most isn't the event itself, but the people you shared it with. I hope we can get back to those quiet Sundays soon.
