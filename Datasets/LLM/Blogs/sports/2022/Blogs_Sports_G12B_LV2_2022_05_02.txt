## The Unexpected Lesson From a Losing Season

It’s funny, isn’t it? How much emotional energy we pour into sports. Last year, 2022, my local high school’s basketball team – the ones I’ve religiously followed since my kid started playing – had a rough go of it. Really rough. They lost more games than they won.

Honestly, I felt deflated. I’d pictured playoff runs, maybe even a deep tournament push. Instead, it was a season of close calls and frustrating defeats. But something shifted for me watching them persevere. It wasn't about the wins and losses anymore. It was about the grit, the practices, the way they supported each other even when things were tough. 

My kid, especially, showed incredible resilience. He didn't get as much playing time as he wanted, but he kept pushing, kept learning. And that, I realized, was a victory in itself. It taught me a valuable lesson about focusing on effort and growth, regardless of the scoreboard. Sometimes, the real win is just showing up and giving it your all.
