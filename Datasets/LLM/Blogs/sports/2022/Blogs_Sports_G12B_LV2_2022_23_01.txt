Fall Saturdays in Iowa. There's a certain magic to them, isn't there? The crisp air, the smell of concession stand food, the roar of the crowd… and, of course, the sound of the marching band. It’s a tradition, a heartbeat of college football.

This year, though, something felt a little…off. Hearing about the scheduling conflicts and the band absences during the Iowa-Iowa State game – "El Classico," as some call it – got me thinking. Those bands represent something bigger than just a team; they embody the spirit of the university. And when that spirit is diminished, even in a small way, it’s a reminder that even traditions aren't immune to the challenges of modern life. Hopefully, they'll find a way to keep that powerful echo resonating loud and clear for years to come. What traditions do *you* miss the most when they’re disrupted?
