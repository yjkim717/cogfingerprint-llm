**The Unexpected Joy of Passing**

Honestly, 2022 was a weird year for my local rugby team. We were good – consistently in the playoffs – but something felt…off. We were so focused on winning, on dominating, that we’d forgotten the simple joy of *playing* together. We were all about the try, the scrum, the points. 

Then, during a particularly frustrating loss, our captain, Liam, pulled us aside. He talked about passing, not just to score, but to keep the momentum going, to build something as a team. It shifted everything. 

Suddenly, supporting each other, a well-timed pass, a shared laugh – those became just as important as the final score. We still wanted to win, but it was about *how* we won, and who we were doing it with. It reminded me a bit of what I’m looking for in a clan – good company, a shared goal, and a whole lot of friendly competition.
