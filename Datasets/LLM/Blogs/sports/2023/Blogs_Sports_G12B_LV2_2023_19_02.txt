**Title: The Unexpected Calm Before the Chaos**

Remember that feeling right before a big race? That frantic energy, the butterflies doing acrobatics? I used to chase that feeling, convinced it was the key to peak performance. This year, though, things shifted.

I started experimenting with mindfulness, specifically focusing on my breath. Sounds a little woo-woo for a runner, right? But honestly, it's been a game-changer. It's not about *eliminating* the pre-race jitters, it's about learning to navigate them.

There’s a strange calm that comes from really *feeling* your breath, from noticing the rise and fall of your chest. It’s like an anchor in a storm. And it's flowed over into my training too. I'm finding a surprising stillness, a clarity of focus, even when pushing my limits.

It’s made me realize that strength isn't just about muscles; it’s about the mental game, and a lot of that starts with a simple, steady breath. Who knew finding quiet could actually make me faster?
