## The Benchwarmers Are Back (and I'm Feeling It)

Remember when everything was purely virtual? For a while, in 2020 and 2021, it felt like the whole world was playing a game from their living rooms.  My fantasy football league, my book club, even catching a workout – all done remotely. It was…convenient. Comfortable, even.

But lately, I’ve been noticing a shift.  My local rec league basketball team, which had gone completely online for scheduling and communication, is pushing for more in-person practices.  The coach says we need that court time, that energy, to really gel. He's right.  The virtual strategy sessions were fine, but you can’t replicate the feel of a real drill, the quick adjustments on the fly.

It's mirroring what I'm hearing about big companies like PwC – bringing folks back into the office, at least part-time. Seems like the "all-virtual" team isn’t always the winning one.  There's something about the shared space, the spontaneous collaboration...the *presence* that gets lost in translation.  I'm a little apprehensive about more practices, but honestly? I'm also looking forward to it.  Maybe being back on the bench, alongside the team, will actually make us better players.
