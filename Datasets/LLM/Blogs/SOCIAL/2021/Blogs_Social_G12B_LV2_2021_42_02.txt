## The Quiet Space Between Aisles

It’s strange, the things that stick with you. This year, 2021, has been full of so many big, loud shifts, hasn’t it? But sometimes, it's the quiet absences that hit the hardest.

We lost a regular at the neighborhood market this past month. Mr. Henderson. He came in every Tuesday, rain or shine, for a newspaper and a specific brand of granola. It wasn't a big purchase, but it was *his* purchase. We’d chat briefly - about the weather, the local baseball team, just the simple things. 

I know it sounds silly to grieve the loss of someone you barely knew, but those little routines, those tiny connections… they build up. They’re the quiet anchors in a world that feels increasingly adrift. The drive-thru lines, the corner markets – these places become more than just transaction points. They’re woven into the fabric of our lives, and the people we see there become familiar faces, even if we don't know their stories. 

Now, Tuesdays feel a little emptier. A little quieter. It's a reminder that even the smallest spaces can hold the biggest of losses, and that connection, in all its forms, is something precious.
