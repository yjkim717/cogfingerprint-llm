## The Curious Case of the Really Long Blockbuster

Anyone else notice something strange about modern movies? We're obsessed with blockbusters, with explosions and spectacle, yet so few go *really* long. I was just thinking about *Ford v Ferrari* – a glorious, nearly three-hour ride! It proved audiences can handle a substantial runtime if the story’s compelling. It got me wondering: are we afraid of commitment when it comes to films? Do we need shorter, snappier entertainment in our increasingly short-attention-span world? What are your favorite epic films?
