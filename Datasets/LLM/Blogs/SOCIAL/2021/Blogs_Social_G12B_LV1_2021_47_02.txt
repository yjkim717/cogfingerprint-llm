## The Algorithm Knows I'm Supposed to Be *Something* More

It's 2021, and I feel…bleh. Like, genuinely, profoundly bleh. I know I *should* be excited. The world's (slowly) opening back up, summer's around the corner, and everyone on Instagram seems to be achieving peak personal growth. Seriously, my feed is a highlight reel of sourdough baking, remote cabin getaways, and impressive career leaps. 

And me? I'm staring at a half-finished cup of tea, wondering if I even *like* tea anymore. 

I’ve been wrestling with this feeling of being…stuck for a while now. It’s not like I’m unhappy, exactly. Just…directionless. Like a boat adrift without a sail. I try to picture myself in five years, and my mind draws a blank. It's unsettling. Everyone else seems to have a plan, a passion, a *thing*. They’re building empires, starting businesses, traveling the globe. 

I find myself scrolling through LinkedIn, comparing myself to people I haven’t spoken to in years, and feeling this weird sense of inadequacy. It's ridiculous, I know. Social media is a carefully curated fantasy, but it’s hard not to internalize it. 

The worst part is the nagging feeling that I *should* be doing something more, something *meaningful*. That the algorithm somehow knows I’m supposed to be further along, more accomplished, more…*something*.

Maybe it’s time to log off, turn off the noise, and just…listen. Listen to myself, to what genuinely sparks a flicker of interest, even if it's something small. Maybe that's the first step to finding my own sail. Any thoughts? Anyone else feeling this?
