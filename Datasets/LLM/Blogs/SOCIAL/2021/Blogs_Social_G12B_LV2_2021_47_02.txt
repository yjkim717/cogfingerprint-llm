## The Scroll and the Sigh: When Social Media Mirrors Your Doubt

It’s 2021, and honestly? Some days I feel like I’m wading through molasses. Not in a dramatic, “my life is falling apart” way, but in a quiet, persistent kind of stuck. Like I’m supposed to be *somewhere* with my life, building something, *becoming* something… but the blueprint is missing.

I know I’m not alone in this, right? We’re all supposed to be hustling, achieving, documenting our perfectly curated lives for the world to see. And scrolling through Instagram, it’s easy to feel like everyone else has it figured out. Travel pics, career milestones, picture-perfect families... it’s a constant highlight reel.

But here's the thing: I'm starting to suspect that highlight reel is doing a lot of the heavy lifting. It’s easy to forget that behind every filtered photo, there’s a whole lot of messy, uncertain, and frankly, *un-Instagrammable* reality.

Lately, I’ve been trying to be more mindful of how much time I spend comparing myself. It’s a thief of joy, and a breeding ground for that low-hum anxiety.  Instead of beating myself up for not having a five-year plan, I’m trying to focus on small steps. On being present. On remembering that my journey doesn’t need to look like anyone else’s.

It’s a work in progress, definitely. But recognizing the scroll and the sigh – the connection between social media and that feeling of being lost – has been a surprisingly helpful first step. Maybe we all need to give ourselves a little more grace, and a little less screen time.
