## The Algorithm Made Me Miss My Grandma

It's weird, isn't it, how much of our lives are now filtered through algorithms? I realized it properly last week when I was scrolling through Instagram, trying to distract myself from…well, everything 2021 seemed to be throwing at us. Pandemic fatigue was *real*. Political tensions were high. And I was just…tired.

Suddenly, an ad popped up. It was for a genealogy website. Not a random ad, mind you. It was tailored. Apparently, based on my recent searches (mostly related to researching old family photos) and the groups I’m in (lots of history buffs, it turns out), the algorithm decided I needed to reconnect with my roots. 

It was strangely poignant. I hadn't really thought about my grandma in a while, beyond a fleeting memory of her apple pie. The ad sparked a wave of nostalgia, a yearning for simpler times and the comfort of family. I ended up spending an hour looking through old photos, feeling a genuine pang of loss.

It made me wonder: are these algorithms actually *helping* us? Or are they just exploiting our vulnerabilities, feeding us precisely what we’re already feeling, isolating us further in our own curated echo chambers? Maybe connecting with the past isn't about tracing lineage, but about consciously reaching out, beyond the screen, to the people who matter *now*.
