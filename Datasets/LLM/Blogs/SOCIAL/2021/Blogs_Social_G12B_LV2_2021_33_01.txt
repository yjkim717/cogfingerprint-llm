## The Irony of the Open Road (and a Whole Lot of Red Lights)

Remember 2021? It felt like we were *all* just desperate to get somewhere, *anywhere*, after being cooped up. And sometimes, that desperation manifested in really frustrating ways. I was reminded of that recently by a story I heard – a driver, clearly in a rush, decided to aggressively speed and pull off some seriously questionable illegal passes. Including, unbelievably, right past a police officer.

You can almost picture the smug satisfaction they probably felt, right? Until they hit a wall of traffic. Just… stuck. 

It struck me as such a perfect little parable for so much of what we were experiencing. This frantic push to get ahead, to bypass the rules, the delays… and ultimately, it gets you nowhere. The irony is thick, isn't it? We all want to feel like we’re moving forward, but sometimes, the hardest part is just accepting the wait, the flow. Maybe 2021 taught us a little something about patience, even if it was a hard lesson learned behind the wheel.
