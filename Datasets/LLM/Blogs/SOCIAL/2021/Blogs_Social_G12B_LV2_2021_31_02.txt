## The Silent Language & the Power of Description

It’s funny, isn’t it? How much we rely on visuals these days. Someone recently reached out, trying to recall an ASL sign – just a memory of hand shapes and movements, no video. It got me thinking about communication beyond the spoken word, beyond pictures. 

ASL (American Sign Language) is a beautiful, complex language, and relying solely on dominant hand or non-dominant hand movements to convey meaning is amazing. It reminded me that connection isn’t *always* about seeing. Sometimes, it’s about the power of descriptive language, of painting a picture with words, and the willingness to truly *listen* – even when the message is silent.
