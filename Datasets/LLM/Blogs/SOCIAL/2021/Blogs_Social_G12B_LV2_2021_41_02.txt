## The Quiet Rebellion of Not Loving Summer

It's July, and everywhere I look, it's sunshine, beaches, and "best. summer. ever." posts. Honestly? It’s a little overwhelming. And, if I’m being completely honest, I’ve always felt a weird, low-level anxiety simmering during the summer months.

It’s not that I *don’t* like warm weather, per se. It’s just… everything feels so… exposed. Clothes are lighter, expectations are higher. Suddenly, you're supposed to be effortlessly glowing and radiating joy, right? And if you're not? Well, that feels like a failure.

2021 has been… a lot. We’ve all been through a lot. And the pressure to bounce back, to *thrive* in this post-lockdown world, feels immense. I find myself scrutinizing my body more, comparing myself to the perfectly curated feeds of influencers frolicking on the beach.  It’s exhausting.

I’m trying to let go of that. To give myself permission to *not* love summer. To be okay with staying inside with a good book, or just feeling… quiet. Maybe this year, my rebellion is simply refusing to participate in the relentless pursuit of the “perfect” summer. Maybe it's just about being kind to myself, and letting the sunshine do its thing without needing to perform for anyone.

Anyone else feel this? Let me know in the comments.
