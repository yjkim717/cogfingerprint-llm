It’s 2021, and I’m watching *My 600lb Life*, wondering why grocery delivery isn’t their go-to. Then it hits me: maybe it’s not just about convenience. For some, that trip to the store is one of the few times they leave the house, a fragile connection to the outside world. The pandemic made us all crave human contact, even the awkward kind. Sometimes, the hardest part of change isn’t the food—it’s letting go of the small rituals that make us feel seen.
