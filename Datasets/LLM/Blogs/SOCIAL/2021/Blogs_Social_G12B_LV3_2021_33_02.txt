## Is This All There Is? The Quiet Panic of Turning 21

It's 2021, and I’m staring down the barrel of… well, adulthood. Officially. Twenty-one. It *sounds* grown-up, right? Like I should have it all figured out. Instead, I’m wrestling with a weird, heavy feeling. It’s not sadness, exactly, more like… a low-hum anxiety about the future.

The biggest fear? Regret. The thought of looking back in, say, twenty years and realizing I’ve spent my prime chasing a career, sacrificing experiences and connections for a paycheck. Burnout looms large in my mind, a monster fueled by hustle culture and the constant pressure to *do more*.

I see so many people my age already talking about work-life balance, and honestly, it feels like a mythical creature. How do you *actually* achieve it when the world screams at you to be constantly productive?  And then there's this creeping fear of aging – not in a superficial way, but the realization that youth is fleeting.  These years, *right now*, are precious.

I don’t have the answers, not by a long shot. But I’m trying to be more intentional. To say ‘yes’ to things that genuinely spark joy, even if they don't look impressive on a resume. To cultivate the relationships that truly matter. Maybe, just maybe, I can quiet that anxious voice and actually *live* a little.
