## The Blank Page Stared Back (and My Phone Didn't Help)

Okay, real talk. I jumped on the bullet journaling bandwagon a few weeks ago, fueled by Pinterest and the promise of a super-organized, aesthetically pleasing life. And… it’s been harder than I thought. That initial burst of enthusiasm? It faded fast when faced with a blank page and the nagging feeling I *should* be writing something profound.

It’s funny, isn’t it? We’re bombarded with content constantly – scrolling, liking, commenting – yet finding the time (and the *desire*) to create something just for myself feels… daunting. I found myself reaching for my phone instead, a familiar comfort. But then I realized, maybe that's exactly what I was trying to escape. This journal is about *me*, not an audience.  Baby steps, right? Just one little task, one little thought...
