**Lost in the Echoes of Connection**

Okay, so I’ve been doing a *lot* of thinking lately about connection, and it feels weirdly linked to this massive, sprawling game I just finished – Baldur’s Gate 3. Seriously, that game is a rabbit hole. But it's not just the epic battles or the gorgeous world that’s stuck with me; it’s the way it forces you to confront the consequences of your choices, and how those choices ripple outwards.

I realized something while playing: we’re all, in a way, navigating a version of Avernus – constantly making decisions that impact the people around us, often without fully understanding the fallout.  Like my character, I sometimes prioritized immediate goals over building genuine bonds. 

This year, 2023, feels like a year of trying to be more intentional about *that*.  Scrolling through endless feeds, it’s easy to feel disconnected, to measure worth by likes and comments. But lately, I've been trying to shift my focus to smaller, more meaningful connections – a real conversation with a friend, volunteering, even just truly *seeing* someone across the room. 

It’s messy, and it’s not always easy. But maybe, just maybe, building a little “Waterdeep” – a safe, supportive space – starts with a single, mindful interaction.
