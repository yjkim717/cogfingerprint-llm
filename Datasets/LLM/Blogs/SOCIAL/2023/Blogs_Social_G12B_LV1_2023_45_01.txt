>## The Weight of Expectation (and a Suitcase)

It's 2023, and everything feels… different. Gas prices, groceries, even the little things sting a bit more. Recently, I was on a Royal Caribbean cruise, and the automatic bag delivery service brought up a surprisingly weighty question: tipping. How much *is* appropriate these days? It’s a small thing, but that $5-$10 per bag felt like another little pinch amidst rising costs. Is it genuine appreciation, or a lingering expectation rooted in older customs? Just thinking out loud – what are your thoughts on tipping in a post-inflation world?
