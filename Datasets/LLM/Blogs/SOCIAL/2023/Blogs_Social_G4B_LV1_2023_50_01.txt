**The 'Am I the Only One?' Trap**

Okay, be honest – how many times this year have you posted “Am I the only one who thinks [insert mildly controversial opinion here]?” on Reddit? It’s a reflex, right? A little dopamine hit of validation. But I’ve been thinking about it lately. It feels…performative. 

Like, it’s a classic figure of speech, a way to frame an unpopular opinion. But are we *actually* seeking genuine connection, or just looking for an echo chamber? It’s easy to feel seen, but is that truly fulfilling? Maybe the bravery lies in sharing, not in *asking* if anyone else feels the same.
