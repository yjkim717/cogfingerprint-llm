"Finding a Home in a New Land: A Struggle for International Students in Germany"

As I navigated the bustling streets of Dusseldorf in 2024, I couldn't help but feel a pang of frustration. As an older international student, I was taken aback by the challenges I faced in securing affordable accommodation. Landlords seemed to be fixated on two things: a pristine Schufa report and a youthful vigor. My credit report, a relic of a different country, was deemed insufficient, and my age, a mere number, became a liability.

I spent countless hours scouring WG-Gesucht, only to be met with rejection after rejection. It dawned on me that I wasn't alone in this struggle. Many international students face similar hurdles, forced to navigate a complex web of credit checks and age biases. As we strive for a more inclusive society, it's time we rethink our approach to housing. After all, shouldn't a home be a haven, not a privilege reserved for the few?
