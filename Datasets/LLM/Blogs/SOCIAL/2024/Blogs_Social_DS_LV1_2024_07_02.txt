The notification pinged just as my shift ended: my pre-order of Garden Home’s self-titled LP had finally arrived. In a world of endless digital streams, there’s something profoundly social about holding a physical album from a band like Kerosene Heights, knowing other fans in Wisconsin and beyond are unboxing the same art. It creates a silent community, a shared anticipation for the same first listen. It’s a small, tangible connection in a disconnected year. Now, to drop the needle and let the post-hardcore cleanse the day away.
