**The Quiet Rebellion of Choosing Not**

It’s not about hating families, you know? It’s about recognizing that the prevailing narrative – that having children is the ultimate measure of success, happiness, or even *humanity* – feels incredibly limiting. I’ve been wrestling with this for a while, and the more I see, the more I’m convinced that valuing autonomy is just as, if not *more*, important than the deeply ingrained societal expectation of parenthood.

I keep encountering this assumption that anyone who isn’t actively building a family is somehow lacking empathy. But I think it’s the opposite. Choosing a childfree life requires a huge amount of empathy – empathy for yourself, for the planet, and for the incredible privilege of dedicating your energy and resources elsewhere. 

There’s a strange conservatism at play here, too, a resistance to the constant, joyful noise of the “shoulds” of modern life. It's about carving out a different path, one built on intentionality and a fierce belief in living life on *your* terms.  And frankly, it feels pretty damn good.
