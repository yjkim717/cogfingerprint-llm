## The Unexpected Warmth of a Steel Heart: Finding Connection in Digital Worlds

It’s funny, isn’t it? How we often seek connection in the real world, while sometimes finding it in the most unexpected places – like a video game. I just finished my first playthrough of Baldur's Gate 3, and I'm still processing it. I went in intending to play a classic, charming rogue, but somehow ended up with a surprisingly fierce, tank-focused Shadowheart, and… I fell for her.

Now, Shadowheart isn’t exactly known for being warm and fuzzy. She’s guarded, secretive, and carries a lot of baggage. But building her as a Trickery Cleric, focusing on protecting my party, seemed to chip away at those walls, revealing a surprising strength and vulnerability. It felt…real.

It made me think about how often we present a curated version of ourselves, even to the people closest to us. Maybe it’s easier to let someone see your defenses down when they’re pixels on a screen.  Or maybe it's just a reminder that genuine connection, in whatever form it takes, requires vulnerability and a willingness to protect what you value.  And sometimes, those lessons come from the most surprising places. What about you? Where have you found unexpected connection lately?
