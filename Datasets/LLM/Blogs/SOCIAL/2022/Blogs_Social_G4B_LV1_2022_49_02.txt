**The Algorithm and My Anger**

Okay, so 2022 was… a year. Between the constant doom-scrolling and trying to figure out TikTok dances, I found myself obsessing over *The Sims 4* again.  It sounds ridiculous, I know, but I stumbled upon this crazy “autonomous killing” mod and, honestly, it tapped into something weird. 

I created a Sim – they called her Pancakes – and she just… kept being awful.  Constantly stealing, insulting, generally being a digital nightmare.  Instead of just letting it go, I built this elaborate “revenge mission” in my game, a meticulously planned series of events to make her suffer. 

It felt petty, I admit.  But it was also strangely satisfying.  It made me think about how easily we get frustrated with online interactions, and how sometimes, even in a simulated world, we crave a little control and a way to… well, *punish* perceived wrongs.  Maybe it's a reflection of our own anxieties, huh?
