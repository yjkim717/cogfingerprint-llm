It’s 2022, and I just finished Rick Riordan’s latest book—wow. For the first time, I saw a teenage girl protagonist not just leading the charge, but doing it while navigating something as real and rarely mentioned in adventure stories: her period. No hushed tones, no shame, just a normal part of her life while she’s busy saving the day.

It hit me how powerful that is. Growing up, periods were this secret we weren’t supposed to talk about, especially in heroic tales. Seeing it normalized in such a popular series feels like a quiet revolution. It tells young readers that leadership and strength aren’t separate from our lived experiences—they’re part of them. Here’s to more stories that aren’t afraid to be real.
