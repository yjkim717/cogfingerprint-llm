**Title: The Wilderness Within: When Leaving the Fight Doesn't Mean Leaving the Risk**

Remember that moment you hit the Wilderness in RuneScape? That sudden shift in the music, the palpable tension… knowing anything could happen? It’s a bizarre feeling, isn’t it? To *choose* to walk into a place designed for conflict.

Lately, I’ve been thinking about that feeling outside of the game. We curate our online lives, carefully opting out of arguments, unfollowing negativity, building walls around our digital selves. We think that means we’re safe. But is it really?

Even when we’re “opting out,” the potential for impact remains. A misinterpreted comment, a carelessly worded post… it can still sting. It can still feel like a sudden, unwanted PvP encounter.

2022 has felt like a year where those unexpected blows have landed frequently. Maybe it's just me, but I feel like we’re all walking around a bit more vulnerable, even when we're trying our best to stay out of the “Wilderness.”  It’s a reminder that sometimes, choosing to disengage doesn’t guarantee immunity to consequence.
