## That MLK Day Dinner & A Little Bit of History

A friend recently asked me a question that really stopped me in my tracks: "Is it okay to make soul food on Martin Luther King Jr. Day?" He genuinely loves the food, eats it often, and wanted to share it with friends. But the timing felt... complicated.

And he's right to feel that. MLK Day is about remembering Dr. King's fight for civil rights, a struggle born from deep systemic injustice. Soul food itself is inextricably linked to that history – a testament to resilience and ingenuity in the face of unimaginable hardship, developed by enslaved people and African Americans in the South.

It's not about *not* enjoying the food. It *is* about awareness. It’s about understanding the history, acknowledging the origin, and celebrating the culture it comes from, not just reducing it to a trendy meal. This year, in 2020, as we grapple with ongoing conversations about race and equity, it feels even more important to approach these things with thoughtfulness and respect. Maybe a moment of reflection alongside that delicious mac and cheese is a good place to start.
