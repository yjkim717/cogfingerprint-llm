It’s 2020, and I’m stuck in my apartment, escaping into the stunning world of *Half-Life: Alyx*. But there’s a catch: aiming the pistol reflex sight is a nightmare. The game demands you use your dominant eye, a concept I’d never considered. I don’t really have one.

It got me thinking—how often do we assume everyone fits a “dominant” mold? In a year of isolation, we’re all navigating a world not built for our individual quirks. That virtual struggle became a small, personal lesson in empathy. We all see the world a little differently, and sometimes, it’s the systems—not us—that need to adjust.
