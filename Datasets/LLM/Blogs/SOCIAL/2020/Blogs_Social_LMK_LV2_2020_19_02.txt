However, what disturbed me was the rapidity with which the narrative spiralled out of control, with many jumping to conclusions and spreading unverified claims.

As the news broke, social media platforms were flooded with tributes to Sushant, and rightly so. But soon, the conversation took a dark turn. Conspiracy theories began to emerge, with many accusing the Bollywood industry of being complicit in his death. The speculation was fuelled by the public's insatiable appetite for sensationalism, and social media platforms, in their quest for engagement, gave oxygen to these unverified claims.

What struck me was how quickly people were willing to believe and share unsubstantiated theories, without stopping to consider the harm they might cause. The Sushant Singh Rajput saga became a prime example of how social media can be both a blessing and a curse. While it brought people together in their grief, it also created an environment where misinformation could spread like wildfire.

As I reflected on this phenomenon, I couldn't help but think about the broader implications. The Sushant Singh Rajput case highlighted the need for a more nuanced conversation around mental health in India. Rather than getting bogged down in conspiracy theories, we should be using this opportunity to discuss the systemic issues that contribute to mental health struggles in our society.

The Sushant Singh Rajput saga serves as a stark reminder of the power of social media and our collective responsibility to wield it wisely.
