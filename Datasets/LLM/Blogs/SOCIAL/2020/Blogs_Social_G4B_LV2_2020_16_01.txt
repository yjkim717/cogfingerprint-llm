**The Quiet Hum of Anxiety**

It’s funny, isn’t it? How sometimes the biggest anxieties aren’t about grand, sweeping disasters. They’re about the slow, persistent drip of something unsettling. That’s how it felt for a while last year, honestly. A quiet hum of anxiety that settled over my street, and eventually, over me.

But it escalated.  The neighbor disputes started, pointed arguments about “dangerous dogs” and “lack of control.”  And the worst part? Animal Control just… didn't seem to *do* anything.  Calls went unanswered, visits were infrequent, and the feeling of helplessness grew with each passing week.

It was about the potential for a dog attack, the potential for a neighbor to lose it, the potential for *me* to be caught in the middle of it all.  It became a real concern for my personal safety. 

It’s strange, isn’t it, how easily a sense of community can erode when basic safety isn't prioritized?  We’re supposed to look out for each other, right?  And yet, there I was, feeling utterly alone in my worry, watching a situation simmer and threaten to boil over. 

Looking back, 2020 felt like a year of so many fragile moments.  We were all grappling with uncertainty, with isolation.
