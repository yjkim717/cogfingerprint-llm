## The Quiet Dignity of Showing Up

Remember 2020? It felt like a constant barrage of reasons to just… quit. From cancelled plans to anxieties swirling everywhere, the urge to tap out was real. I saw it a lot, honestly, in online communities, in conversations with friends. People ghosting, dropping out, just disappearing. And while I totally *got* it, a part of me felt a pang of sadness.

It reminded me of something I learned playing Rocket League (yes, really!). There were tournaments where our team was getting absolutely demolished. The temptation to forfeit, to just save face and the remaining time, was HUGE. But we kept playing. We practiced passing, tried new strategies, even just laughed at our mistakes.

It wasn't about winning. It was about showing up, putting in the effort, and maintaining a little bit of dignity, even when things felt hopeless. And that lesson? It felt surprisingly relevant to *everything* that year. Sometimes, just showing up and trying is a victory in itself. It's a quiet act of resilience, and honestly, it’s something we all need to remember.
