## The Echo Chamber of Anxiety – And What I’m Seeing Lately

It's 2020, and if you’re anything like me, you’re feeling… a lot. The news cycle is relentless, the world feels perpetually on edge, and honestly, sometimes it feels like everyone’s anxieties are colliding in a giant, swirling vortex. I’ve been thinking a lot about how we’re all feeding off each other’s fears lately.

I stumbled across a really weird thought experiment the other day - a fictional concept of a power that draws on and amplifies people's deepest fears. Imagine a gas, almost invisible, that latches onto your worries, your insecurities, and then *recreates* them, not just in your head, but manifesting them around you. It’s a little unsettling to think about, right?

And it got me thinking… aren't we doing something similar online? Social media, especially, can feel like that gas. We share our anxieties about the future, about health, about politics, and then we’re bombarded with similar anxieties from everyone else. It’s an echo chamber of worry, a constant reinforcement of the things that scare us.

I've noticed it in my own behavior, too. I catch myself spiraling down rabbit holes of worst-case scenarios, fueled by what I’ve seen online. I find myself questioning things I wouldn't have before, just because I've been exposed to a constant stream of negativity.

Maybe we need to be more mindful of the “gas” we’re releasing into the digital atmosphere. Maybe a little more focus on the good, a little more conscious effort to dial down the fear-mongering, could help us all breathe a little easier. It’s a strange thought, but maybe cultivating a little digital serenity is more important now than ever.
