The year 2020 was already a blur of uncertainty when our D&D group dove into a homebrew module. I played a Dragonborn Cleric, convinced a fragile hatchling was Tiamat’s avatar—my devotion to resurrect Io absolute. But after a virtual session, a friend gently noted my character’s fervor mirrored our collective search for purpose in isolation. That head trauma backstory? Suddenly, it felt less like fantasy and more like a metaphor for how we all cope, desperately seeking meaning when the world feels fractured.
