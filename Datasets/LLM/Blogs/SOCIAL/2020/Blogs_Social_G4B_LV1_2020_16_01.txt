**The Quietest Kind of Chaos**

Okay, so 2020 was… a lot, right? Between the pandemic and everything else, I was just trying to keep my head above water. But there was this quiet, simmering tension happening right outside my front door that I couldn’t ignore, and honestly, it’s still bothering me.

It started subtly. Just a few snarling glances from a neighbor’s golden retriever, Max. Then the little things – a trampled flowerbed, a shredded garbage can. It escalated quickly. Max wasn’t just being a rambunctious dog; he was genuinely aggressive, and it was clear he was being poorly managed. 

I brought it to animal control, of course. You do. But it felt like shouting into a void. The forms were endless, the responses slow, and the feeling of helplessness just grew.  It culminated in a pretty terrifying incident – Max actually lunged at a kid riding his bike. Thankfully, no serious injuries, but it was a stark reminder of the potential danger. 

Eventually, Max was…gone. The neighbor claimed he'd "run away," but I’m not so sure. It felt like animal control had simply given up. 

It’s weird, isn’t it? How a series of small frustrations can build into something so unsettling.
