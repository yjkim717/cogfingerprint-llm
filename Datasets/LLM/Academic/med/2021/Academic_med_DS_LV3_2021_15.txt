This 2021 case report examines a 44-year-old female presenting with symptomatic pulmonary hamartoma, deviating from its classic asymptomatic presentation in elderly demographics. The study delineates the diagnostic challenges through computed tomography and histopathological confirmation, highlighting atypical features including cough and localized pain. It underscores that symptomatic benign lung tumors warrant expanded differential diagnoses across age groups. Clinical management considerations emphasize individualized surgical intervention based on symptom severity and tumor characteristics. This case contributes to refining diagnostic protocols for pulmonary hamartomas, advocating for heightened vigilance in atypical presentations to optimize patient-specific therapeutic strategies.
