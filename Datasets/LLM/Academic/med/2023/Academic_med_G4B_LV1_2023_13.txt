**Dietary Exposure to Bisphenol A Exceeds Tolerable Daily Intake: Implications for Immune System Vulnerability**

**Abstract:**  Despite established guidelines, recent investigations suggest a significant underestimation of the public health risk posed by bisphenol A (BPA) exposure. This study synthesizes existing epidemiological and toxicological data to re-evaluate BPA’s dietary contribution and its potential impact on human health.  Analysis reveals that current Tolerable Daily Intake (TDI) values, established by regulatory agencies, fail to account for widespread dietary intake of BPA from various food packaging materials and consumer products.  We demonstrate that estimated daily BPA exposure, derived from representative dietary surveys, consistently surpasses the currently defined TDI by a considerable margin.  Specifically, we highlight the potential for chronic, low-level BPA exposure to disrupt immune system homeostasis. Emerging evidence implicates BPA as an endocrine disruptor, capable of modulating immune cell function and increasing susceptibility to inflammatory responses.  These findings necessitate a critical reassessment of BPA’s risk profile and underscore the urgent need for revised TDI values and further investigation into the long-term consequences of persistent BPA exposure on immune system integrity and overall public health.
