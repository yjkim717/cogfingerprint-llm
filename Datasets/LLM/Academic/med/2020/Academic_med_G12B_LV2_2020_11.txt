**Title: Early Radiologic Management Strategies and Challenges in COVID-19: Experience of the Iranian Society of Radiology Consultant Group (2020)**

**Abstract**

The emergence of the novel severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) and the resultant coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) pandemic presented unprecedented challenges to healthcare systems globally.  Given the critical role of radiology in the diagnosis, monitoring, and management of COVID-19 pneumonia, the Iranian Society of Radiology (ISR) rapidly established a COVID-19 Consultant Group in early 2020 to synthesize emerging evidence and provide practical guidance to Iranian radiologists. This paper summarizes the preliminary experience and evolving strategies of this Consultant Group during the initial phase of the pandemic.

The group’s activities focused on developing standardized imaging protocols for chest computed tomography (CT) in suspected and confirmed COVID-19 cases, emphasizing the utility of low-dose CT protocols to minimize radiation exposure.  Key areas of consensus included the characterization of pulmonary findings, including ground-glass opacities, consolidation, and the distribution patterns associated with varying disease severity.  Furthermore, the Consultant Group addressed the challenges of differentiating COVID-19 pneumonia from other lung diseases prevalent within the Iranian population, such as tuberculosis and fungal infections, particularly in resource-limited settings.

This report details the evolving understanding of the radiologic spectrum of COVID-19 and the practical considerations for image interpretation in a context of high patient volume and limited access to advanced imaging technologies in certain regions of Iran. The initial strategies also addressed the need for rapid turnaround times for reporting, while maintaining diagnostic accuracy.  The experience highlighted the importance of collaborative efforts and rapid adaptation of guidelines based on evolving scientific literature and clinical observations, as well as the logistical difficulties encountered in disseminating these guidelines across a diverse geographical landscape.  Future work will focus on evaluating the long-term impact of these initial protocols on patient outcomes and refining imaging strategies as our understanding of COVID-19 continues to evolve.
