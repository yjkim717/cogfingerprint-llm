**Abstract: Computational Analysis of Operational Efficiency in Multi-Lateral Development Banks: A Panel Data Approach with Generalized Method of Moments**

This study investigates the impact of competitive dynamics and institutional scale on the operational efficiency of development banks within the BRICS (Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa) economic bloc. Leveraging a panel dataset spanning 2010-2019, we employ a Generalized Method of Moments (GMM) estimation framework to mitigate endogeneity concerns prevalent in cross-sectional analyses. Our efficiency metric, derived from Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA) applied to publicly available financial data, quantifies the banks' relative performance in allocating resources. We hypothesize that increased competition amongst BRICS banks, measured through Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) calculations of their lending portfolios, will exert downward pressure on operational costs. Further, we explore the relationship between bank size (total assets) and efficiency, testing for economies of scale, potentially influenced by network effects and computational infrastructure investments.  Preliminary results suggest a non-linear relationship between size and efficiency, with diminishing returns observed beyond a certain asset threshold.  Finally, we incorporate macroeconomic development indicators (GDP per capita, human development index) as control variables to isolate the effects of competition and scale.  This research contributes to the field of computational economics by providing a rigorous, data-driven assessment of BRICS bank performance and offers insights relevant to the design of policies promoting efficient international development finance.
