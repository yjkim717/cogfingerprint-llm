**Abstract**

Generating realistic and contextually consistent scenes containing human figures remains a significant challenge in computer graphics and artificial intelligence. This paper presents a novel generative adversarial network (GAN) architecture for scene generation conditioned on human pose, addressing limitations in existing approaches regarding pose fidelity and scene diversity. Our method leverages a StyleGAN2 backbone, modified to incorporate explicit pose conditioning through an adaptive feature injection mechanism. This allows for fine-grained control over the generated scene’s layout and object arrangement in response to a specified human pose.  Crucially, we introduce a large-scale dataset of paired human poses and corresponding scenes, facilitating robust training and enabling the generation of complex, visually plausible environments.  Experimental results demonstrate that our approach produces significantly improved pose adherence compared to state-of-the-art methods, while maintaining a high degree of scene realism and diversity.  Furthermore, qualitative and quantitative evaluations, including metrics assessing pose consistency and scene plausibility, confirm the efficacy of our pose conditioning strategy. This work represents a substantial advancement in controllable scene generation, with potential applications spanning virtual reality, robotics, and human-computer interaction.
