**Abstract: Data-Driven Identification of Terrorist Activity Patterns in Nigeria via Apriori Algorithm Application**

The escalating threat of terrorism necessitates robust analytical approaches to identify emergent patterns and inform preventative strategies. This paper presents a data-driven investigation into terrorist activities within Nigeria utilizing the Global Terrorism Database (GTD). We explore the application of the Apriori algorithm, a well-established association rule mining technique from the field of data mining, to uncover significant co-occurrence relationships among various attributes within the GTD dataset pertaining to Nigeria.  Our methodology involves pre-processing the GTD data, implementing the Apriori algorithm to identify frequent itemsets, and subsequently generating association rules with defined support, confidence, and lift thresholds.  The resulting rules are then analyzed to reveal potential patterns related to attack methods, target types, perpetrator affiliations, and geographic locations.  Preliminary findings suggest the identification of recurring event characteristics indicative of specific operational strategies employed by various actor groups.  This research contributes a novel application of association rule mining to terrorism analysis, offering a computationally efficient approach to inform forensic investigation and potentially enhance predictive capabilities within the context of Nigerian counter-terrorism efforts.  Future work will focus on incorporating temporal dynamics and exploring alternative data mining techniques for enhanced pattern recognition.
