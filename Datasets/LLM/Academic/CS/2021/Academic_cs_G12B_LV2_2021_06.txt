**Abstract: The Influence of Competitive Dynamics, Scale, and Economic Development on the Efficiency of BRICS Development Banks**

This study investigates the relationship between competitive forces, bank size, and underlying economic development levels on the operational efficiency of development banks within the BRICS (Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa) economic bloc. Utilizing a panel dataset encompassing these institutions from 2010 to 2019, we employ a two-stage Generalized Method of Moments (2GMM) estimation technique to address potential endogeneity and ensure robust inference. Our analysis examines how the intensity of competition, measured through indicators such as the number of active development banks and market share concentration, impacts technical and cost efficiency. Furthermore, we assess the role of bank size, operationalized via asset size and total lending volume, in determining efficiency levels, considering potential economies of scale. The model also controls for key development indicators—GDP per capita, human development index, and infrastructure investment—to isolate the effects of the core variables of interest. Preliminary results suggest a nuanced relationship; while increased competition appears to exert a positive influence on efficiency, the impact of bank size is more complex and potentially exhibits diminishing returns. Future research will explore the moderating effects of regulatory frameworks and institutional quality on these relationships and investigate the impact of specific lending portfolios on bank efficiency.
