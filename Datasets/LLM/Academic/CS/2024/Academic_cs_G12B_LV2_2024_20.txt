**Abstract**

The increasing prevalence of high-throughput microscopy in biological research generates vast datasets often characterized by image quality degradation stemming from factors such as noise, blur, and incomplete sampling. These artifacts significantly impede accurate cellular segmentation, a critical prerequisite for quantitative analysis and downstream biological interpretation. This work introduces a novel, integrated workflow leveraging Cellpose3 for image restoration and subsequent cellular segmentation within a single, automated pipeline.  Unlike traditional approaches requiring separate pre-processing and segmentation steps, our method provides a generalized “one-click” solution applicable across diverse microscopy modalities and cell types. Cellpose3's underlying deep learning architecture, trained on a broad range of image features, enables effective denoising and deblurring while simultaneously providing robust segmentation masks. Preliminary results demonstrate improved segmentation accuracy on synthetically degraded datasets compared to standard Cellpose3 application without restoration, suggesting enhanced performance in challenging imaging conditions. Future research will focus on quantitative evaluation across a wider range of biological datasets, exploration of parameter optimization strategies for specific image degradation profiles, and integration with automated data analysis pipelines. This approach represents a significant advancement towards efficient and reliable automated analysis of microscopy data.
