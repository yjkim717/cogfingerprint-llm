**An Ex-Ante Optimal Logarithmic Approximation for Revenue Maximization via a Multi-Dimensional Online Contention Resolution Scheme**

The problem of revenue maximization in settings with subadditive buyers presents a significant challenge in algorithmic mechanism design, particularly when faced with the online nature of many real-world auctions and resource allocation scenarios. Existing online contention resolution schemes (OCRS) often struggle to achieve optimal revenue guarantees, especially as the number of buyers and potential item combinations grows. This paper addresses this limitation by introducing a novel multi-dimensional OCRS specifically tailored for environments characterized by subadditive buyer valuations.  Our approach leverages a sequential item pricing strategy, dynamically adjusting prices based on observed buyer behavior in an online fashion.

We rigorously demonstrate that our proposed OCRS provides an *ex ante* logarithmic approximation factor to the optimal revenue. This represents a substantial improvement over existing single-dimensional OCRS and offers a practical and scalable solution for revenue maximization in complex online environments. The multi-dimensional aspect of our scheme allows for finer-grained allocation and pricing decisions, leading to increased revenue capture.  The logarithmic approximation guarantee is established through a combination of probabilistic analysis and careful design of the contention resolution process.  Furthermore, we provide empirical validation through simulations, illustrating the effectiveness of our scheme compared to benchmark OCRS algorithms. This work contributes to the growing body of research on online revenue optimization and offers a valuable tool for practitioners designing online marketplaces and resource allocation platforms.
