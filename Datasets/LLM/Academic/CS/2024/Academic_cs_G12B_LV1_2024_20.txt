**Abstract: Generalist Deep Learning for Robust Cellular Image Analysis via Cellpose3**

The increasing volume and complexity of microscopy data present significant challenges for automated cellular analysis. Traditional cellular segmentation pipelines often require laborious manual parameter tuning and are highly sensitive to image artifacts stemming from noise, blur, and undersampling. This paper introduces Cellpose3, a novel deep learning framework designed to provide a robust and generalizable 'one-click' solution for simultaneously improving image restoration and cellular segmentation across diverse microscopy modalities. Leveraging a generalist convolutional neural network architecture trained on a large, heterogeneous dataset of cell images, Cellpose3 implicitly learns image priors that facilitate both denoising and enhanced feature extraction. Experimental results across a range of datasets, including fluorescence microscopy, phase contrast, and light sheet microscopy, demonstrate that Cellpose3 achieves competitive or superior segmentation accuracy compared to specialized segmentation algorithms while requiring minimal user intervention.  We further evaluate its efficacy in addressing common image quality issues, showing improved performance in scenarios with limited data and substantial noise, highlighting its potential to democratize advanced image analysis within the biological sciences.
