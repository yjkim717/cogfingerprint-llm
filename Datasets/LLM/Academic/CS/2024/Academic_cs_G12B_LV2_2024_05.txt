**Abstract**

The increasing availability of algorithmic prediction capabilities has spurred interest in their application to resource allocation problems across diverse domains. This paper investigates the conditions under which prediction-based resource allocation strategies outperform traditional, aggregate-level allocation methods. We present a theoretical analysis, supported by simulations, demonstrating that the efficacy of algorithmic predictions is critically dependent on both the degree of inequality among units receiving resources and the magnitude of the intervention budget. Specifically, our findings indicate that prediction-based allocation only yields superior outcomes when unit-level heterogeneity is low and a substantial intervention budget is available to capitalize on predicted individual needs. Conversely, high inequality and limited budgets diminish the advantage of predictive approaches, potentially exacerbating existing disparities. These results highlight a crucial trade-off: while algorithmic predictions offer the potential for targeted resource distribution, their effectiveness is contingent on distributional characteristics and budgetary constraints. Further research should explore the robustness of these findings across different prediction models and allocation mechanisms, and investigate strategies for mitigating the adverse impacts of inequality in prediction-driven resource allocation.
