**Abstract: The Conditional Efficacy of Algorithmic Resource Allocation Under Heterogeneity**

The increasing adoption of algorithmic prediction for resource allocation across diverse domains – from education to public health – necessitates a rigorous theoretical understanding of its efficacy. This paper investigates the conditions under which prediction-based allocation strategies demonstrably outperform traditional, uniform allocation methods. Employing a novel mathematical model incorporating unit-level statistics and a constrained intervention budget, we demonstrate that the effectiveness of algorithmic interventions is critically dependent on the degree of heterogeneity across units. Specifically, we prove that prediction-based allocation yields significant improvements only when inter-unit variance in predicted outcomes is relatively low. Furthermore, our analysis reveals a positive correlation between the intervention budget size and the efficacy of algorithmic approaches; limited budgets exacerbate the impact of heterogeneity, negating predictive gains. These findings challenge the often-assumed universal benefits of algorithmic resource allocation, highlighting the importance of pre-intervention distributional analysis and budget considerations. We conclude with a discussion of methods for mitigating the adverse effects of inequality on algorithmic performance, including fairness-aware prediction techniques and adaptive budget allocation strategies, relevant for ongoing research in algorithmic fairness and resource optimization.
