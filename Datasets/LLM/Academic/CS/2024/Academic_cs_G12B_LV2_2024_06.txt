**Abstract: Assessing the Resilience and Post-COVID Performance of Bangladeshi Commercial Banks: A Data-Driven Analysis**

The COVID-19 pandemic introduced unprecedented systemic shocks to global financial systems, necessitating a rigorous assessment of institutional resilience. This study investigates the impact of the pandemic on Bangladeshi commercial banks, focusing on the interplay between risk, stability, and post-pandemic performance. Utilizing a longitudinal dataset encompassing financial statements and macroeconomic indicators from 2018 to 2023, we employ a quantitative methodology to model the effects of COVID-19-related disruptions. Specifically, we leverage panel data regression techniques, incorporating fixed effects to control for unobserved heterogeneity, and employ time-series analysis of key performance indicators (KPIs) such as Return on Assets (ROA), Net Interest Margin (NIM), and Non-Performing Loan (NPL) ratios.  Furthermore, we utilize Value-at-Risk (VaR) and Expected Shortfall (ES) metrics to quantify and compare risk exposures across a sample of banks before and after the pandemic onset.

Our findings reveal a statistically significant decline in aggregate profitability, evidenced by reduced ROA and NIM, coupled with a demonstrable increase in credit risk, as indicated by elevated NPL ratios during the pandemic period. However, we also observe improvements in operational efficiency, potentially attributable to accelerated digital transformation initiatives.  Importantly, our regression models identify key performance determinants, including capital adequacy ratios, loan diversification, and digital adoption rates, as significant predictors of bank resilience.  The VaR and ES analysis confirms a substantial widening of risk profiles, particularly for banks with concentrated loan portfolios. These results underscore the importance of proactive risk management strategies and regulatory oversight in mitigating the adverse impacts of exogenous shocks. Consequently, this research contributes to the body of knowledge on financial system resilience and provides actionable insights for policymakers and banking practitioners seeking to enhance the stability and performance of commercial banks in emerging economies.
