**Abstract: Algorithmic Risk Assessment in Emerging Market Banking: A Dynamic Panel Approach to BRICS Bank Risk-Taking**

The increasing reliance on algorithmic risk assessment within financial institutions necessitates a rigorous understanding of factors influencing bank risk-taking behavior, particularly within dynamic emerging markets. This study investigates the impact of market competition and revenue diversification on the risk profiles of BRICS (Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa) banks, leveraging a dynamic panel data framework with a Generalized Method of Moments (GMM) estimator. We model bank risk-taking as a function of Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) as a proxy for market concentration, a diversification index capturing the breadth of revenue streams, and control variables including bank size, capital adequacy ratio, and macroeconomic indicators. Our analysis, employing data from 2010-2019, reveals a statistically significant, inverse relationship between market competition (measured by HHI) and risk-taking propensity in BRICS banks, alongside a complex, non-linear effect of revenue diversification.  The findings suggest that while diversification offers potential risk mitigation, excessive reliance on non-traditional revenue streams can introduce unforeseen vulnerabilities.  We contribute to the literature by providing empirical evidence relevant to the design of robust algorithmic risk management systems operating within the context of BRICS economies, highlighting the importance of dynamic competition and diversification strategies in mitigating systemic risk.  Further research should explore the impact of regulatory interventions on these relationships.
