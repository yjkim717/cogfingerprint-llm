**Penetration Testing Resource Allocation via Deferred-Acceptance Auction with Logarithmic Approximation Guarantees**

Abstract:

The increasing complexity of modern software systems necessitates robust and efficient penetration testing methodologies.  Traditional approaches often struggle to optimally allocate diverse testing resources (e.g., specialized tools, skilled analysts, time windows) to maximize vulnerability discovery given budgetary constraints. This paper introduces a novel resource allocation framework for penetration testing grounded in deferred-acceptance (DA) auction mechanisms. We formulate the problem as a combinatorial auction where bids represent the expected vulnerability impact of a given testing effort, and constraints reflect dependencies between tests and resource limitations.  A key contribution is the development of DA-based algorithms that provide a provable logarithmic approximation factor to the optimal allocation, even under complex combinatorial constraints.  We leverage the concept of *virtual values* to mitigate bid manipulation and enhance auction stability.  Empirical evaluations, conducted on synthetic and real-world attack surface models, demonstrate that our approach achieves near-optimal resource utilization while maintaining computational tractability.  Future work will focus on extending this framework to dynamic environments where vulnerability landscapes evolve rapidly, incorporating machine learning techniques for more accurate bid estimation and exploring adaptive auction protocols to further improve approximation guarantees and responsiveness.
