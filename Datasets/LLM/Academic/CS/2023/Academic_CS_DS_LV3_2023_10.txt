The Carimas platform represents a significant advancement in computational medical imaging, enabling multimodal integration of positron emission tomography (PET), computed tomography (CT), and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) datasets. This software framework employs sophisticated algorithms for multidimensional data processing, visualization, and quantitative modeling, with particular efficacy in cardiac perfusion mapping. By facilitating precise spatial and temporal analysis of myocardial blood flow, Carimas addresses critical challenges in cardiovascular diagnostics and treatment planning. Its architecture supports both clinical applications and research investigations, demonstrating how integrated computational approaches can enhance diagnostic accuracy while maintaining methodological rigor. The system's capacity for handling complex tomographic data streams positions it as an essential tool in modern computational medicine, bridging imaging technology with clinical decision-making.
