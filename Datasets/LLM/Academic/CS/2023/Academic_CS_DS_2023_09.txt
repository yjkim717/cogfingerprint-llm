This paper introduces a modular computational framework for multimodal medical image analysis, designed to address the growing complexity of hybrid imaging data in clinical research environments. The system implements unified data structures and processing pipelines for integrating functional and anatomical datasets from PET, CT, and MRI modalities. Through containerized architecture and standardized APIs, the platform enables reproducible quantification of physiological parameters while maintaining compatibility with diverse scanner outputs. Validation studies demonstrate robust performance in cardiac perfusion analysis, showcasing the system's capability to streamline research workflows. The proposed solution represents a significant advancement in computational infrastructure for quantitative medical imaging, facilitating collaborative research across imaging domains.
