**Abstract**

The proliferation of automated vehicles (AVs) necessitates robust and proactive collision avoidance strategies, particularly those leveraging multi-agent cooperation to enhance overall traffic safety. This paper presents a comprehensive survey of existing techniques for cooperative collision avoidance within automated vehicle systems, identifying key limitations and emerging trends in the field. Current approaches, ranging from rule-based systems and Model Predictive Control to decentralized game-theoretic frameworks, often struggle with scalability, adaptability to dynamic environments, and the inherent uncertainties of real-world traffic scenarios.  Recognizing these shortcomings, we propose an AI-enabled framework designed to facilitate efficient and resilient cooperative collision avoidance.  This framework integrates reinforcement learning for adaptive trajectory planning, coupled with a decentralized communication architecture based on vehicular ad-hoc networks (VANETs) to enable rapid information exchange between AVs. Furthermore, we incorporate Bayesian networks to model and reason under uncertainty regarding vehicle states and intentions.  The proposed framework’s architecture aims to improve upon existing methodologies by providing a scalable, adaptive, and robust solution for mitigating collision risks in complex traffic systems. This survey concludes with a discussion of future research directions, including the integration of explainable AI (XAI) to enhance trust and transparency in AV decision-making, and the exploration of federated learning approaches to enable collaborative model training without compromising data privacy.
