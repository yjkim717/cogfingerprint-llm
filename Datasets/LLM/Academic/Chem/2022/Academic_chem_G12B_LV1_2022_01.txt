**Abstract: Experimental Determination of Hydroxyl Radical Diffusion Activation Energy within Interstellar Ice Analogues**

Understanding the dynamics of reactive species within interstellar ices is crucial for accurately modeling astrochemistry and the formation of complex organic molecules in space. This study presents experimental measurements of the activation energy (E<sub>a</sub>) for hydroxyl radical (OH•) diffusion within frozen water ice, a prevalent component of interstellar grain mantles. Employing photostimulated desorption (PSD) techniques on a water ice matrix at cryogenic temperatures (8-15 K), we monitored the release of OH• following pulsed UV irradiation.  Kinetic analysis of the PSD signal, utilizing an Arrhenius-type relationship, allowed for the determination of E<sub>a</sub>. Our findings reveal an activation energy of 0.14 ± 0.02 eV for OH• diffusion within the ice matrix. This relatively low value suggests that OH• mobility is facilitated by pre-existing defects or structural rearrangements within the ice network, impacting its reactivity and influence on subsequent chemical reactions. The implications for the efficiency of radical-radical reactions and the overall chemical evolution of interstellar ices are discussed.
