These findings highlight the importance of considering non-Markovian dynamics when interpreting experimental results in polariton chemistry and offer a pathway towards rationally controlling reaction selectivity through precise manipulation of cavity parameters. This approach provides a novel strategy for modulating reaction pathways and opens new avenues for light-driven chemical synthesis.
