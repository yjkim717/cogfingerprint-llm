**Abstract:**

The accelerating complexity of materials chemistry research necessitates a paradigm shift towards enhanced data management and accessibility. This work investigates the implementation of FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) data principles within the materials chemistry domain, addressing current limitations in data sharing and knowledge discovery. We evaluate the efficacy of existing data repositories, identifying both strengths and areas for improvement concerning metadata standards, data provenance tracking, and long-term preservation.  Beyond repository infrastructure, we explore strategies for fostering a culture of data sharing and rewarding responsible data stewardship within the research community.  Specifically, we propose a tiered framework incorporating standardized ontologies for materials descriptions and automated data validation pipelines to improve interoperability. This framework aims to unlock the potential for machine learning-driven materials design and accelerate the identification of novel materials with targeted properties.  Ultimately, embracing FAIR data practices will be crucial for realizing the full potential of the rapidly expanding materials chemistry landscape and fostering collaborative, data-driven innovation.
