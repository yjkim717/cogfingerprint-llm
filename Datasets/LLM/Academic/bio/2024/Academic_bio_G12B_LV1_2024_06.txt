**Abstract**

The efficient generation of hydrogen via electrochemical water splitting is a critical pathway toward a sustainable energy future. However, the sluggish oxygen evolution reaction (OER) remains a significant bottleneck. This study investigates the impact of cobalt doping on the electrocatalytic performance of strontium irate (SrIrO₃) for OER. We demonstrate that introducing cobalt into the SrIrO₃ lattice strategically modifies the surface electronic structure and oxygen vacancy concentration, resulting in markedly enhanced OER activity. Specifically, Co-doping facilitates the activation of surface lattice oxygen, promoting its participation in the redox processes inherent to OER. Furthermore, computational modeling reveals an optimization of binding energies for key OER intermediates on the Co-doped SrIrO₃ surface, lowering the activation barrier for oxygen release. Electrochemical measurements reveal that Co-doped SrIrO₃ significantly surpasses the performance of benchmark commercial iridium dioxide (IrO₂) electrocatalysts in terms of both activity and stability. These findings highlight the potential of cost-effective, earth-abundant transition metal oxide materials, such as Co-doped SrIrO₃, for accelerating the development of highly efficient and durable water splitting technologies.
