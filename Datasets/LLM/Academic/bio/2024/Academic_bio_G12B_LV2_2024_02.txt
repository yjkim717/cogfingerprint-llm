**Somatostatin and Colorectal Cancer: Modulation of the MAPK/ERK/AKT Pathway and Therapeutic Opportunities within the Tumor Microenvironment**

**Abstract**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) remains a significant global health challenge, necessitating exploration of novel therapeutic strategies. This review synthesizes current knowledge regarding the multifaceted role of somatostatin (SST) and its analogs in modulating CRC progression, with a specific focus on sporadic cases. While traditionally associated with gastroenteropancreatic neuroendocrine tumors (GEP-NETs), emerging evidence highlights SST’s potential antitumor effects in non-neuroendocrine CRC. We examine the intricate signaling pathways influenced by SST, particularly the MAPK/ERK/AKT cascade, a critical regulator of cell proliferation, survival, and metastasis. The review details how SST receptor activation can disrupt this pathway, demonstrating both direct and indirect mechanisms, including alterations in microenvironmental factors.  Furthermore, we discuss the impact of the tumor microenvironment (TME) – encompassing immune cells, vasculature, and extracellular matrix – on SST’s efficacy, highlighting the potential for synergistic therapeutic approaches combining SST analogs with immunotherapies or targeted agents.  Finally, we address current limitations in clinical translation, including variable SST receptor expression and the development of resistance, and propose avenues for future research focused on optimizing SST-based therapies, such as receptor subtype-selective agonists and strategies to overcome TME-mediated immunosuppression.  This review aims to consolidate current understanding and stimulate further investigation into SST as a viable therapeutic modality for CRC in 2024.
