This workforce shortage, identified as a significant impediment to effective healthcare delivery system function, necessitates urgent and strategic intervention. Current fragmentation in AHP training programs, characterized by a lack of curricula uniformity and inconsistent quality control, contributes to sub-optimal workforce preparedness and reduced patient outcomes. Such a council would facilitate standardized curricula development, promote evidence-based training methodologies, and implement robust workforce estimation strategies to align educational output with projected healthcare needs. Furthermore, centralized governance would enable consistent professional registration and continuing professional development, thereby enhancing the quality and accountability of AHP practice. The proposed council represents a crucial step towards strengthening India’s healthcare infrastructure and improving equitable access to high-quality care, particularly given the ongoing pressures revealed by the COVID-19 pandemic and the projected growth in geriatric populations.
