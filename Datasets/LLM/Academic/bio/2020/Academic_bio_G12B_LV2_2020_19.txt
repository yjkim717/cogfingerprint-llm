**Abstract**

The oxygen evolution reaction (OER) represents a critical bottleneck in numerous electrochemical energy conversion and storage technologies, necessitating the development of efficient and robust electrocatalysts. Here, we report a novel heterostructure comprising FeOOH nanoparticles decorated on a LaNiO₃ perovskite support exhibiting significantly enhanced OER activity. Electrochemical and spectroscopic characterization reveal that the synergistic interaction between FeOOH and LaNiO₃ facilitates a mechanism dominated by lattice oxygen oxidation. Specifically, the presence of FeOOH promotes the facile removal of oxygen ions from the LaNiO₃ lattice, a rate-limiting step in many perovskite-based OER catalysts. Furthermore, we observe a concomitant weakening of the Ni-O bonds within the LaNiO₃ structure, potentially attributable to electronic modification induced by the FeOOH decoration. This reduction in bond strength lowers the activation energy for oxygen release, further contributing to the improved catalytic performance. The optimized heterostructure demonstrates a substantial decrease in overpotential compared to pristine LaNiO₃, highlighting a promising strategy for designing high-performance OER electrocatalysts by leveraging synergistic effects between oxide materials. This work contributes to the ongoing effort to identify cost-effective and sustainable alternatives to noble metal-based catalysts for OER applications.
