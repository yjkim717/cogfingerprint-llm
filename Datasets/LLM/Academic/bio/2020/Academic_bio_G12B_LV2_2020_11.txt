**Abstract**

Understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying aging remains a central challenge in biomedical research. While several hallmarks of aging have been proposed, their spatiotemporal dynamics across diverse tissues remain poorly characterized. Here, we present an integrated transcriptomic and proteomic analysis of the *Tabula Muris Senis* (TMS), a comprehensive dataset profiling gene expression and protein abundance across multiple organs in aged (20-24 months) C57BL/6J mice. Utilizing this resource, we identified distinct and temporally evolving signatures of aging within individual organs, revealing organ-specific trajectories of molecular change.  Our analysis demonstrates significant divergence in the relative contributions of established aging hallmarks – including genomic instability, telomere attrition, mitochondrial dysfunction, and altered intercellular communication – across tissues such as the liver, kidney, and heart. Notably, we observed a consistent and robust shift in immune-related gene expression and protein levels across multiple organs, indicative of a systemic age-associated immune dysregulation. Furthermore, correlation analysis between RNA sequencing and proteomics data revealed a complex interplay between transcriptional and translational control during aging, highlighting tissue-specific differences in post-transcriptional regulation. These findings provide a high-resolution molecular atlas of aging in mice, facilitating a deeper understanding of age-related physiological decline and informing the development of targeted interventions aimed at modulating specific aging processes within vulnerable tissues. The identification of conserved immune signatures across organs suggests potential therapeutic strategies targeting systemic immune modulation to promote healthy aging.
