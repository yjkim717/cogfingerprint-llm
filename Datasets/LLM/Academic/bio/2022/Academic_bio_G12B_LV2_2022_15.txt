**The Ghrelin System: A Multifaceted Regulator of Colorectal Cancer Development and Therapeutic Potential**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) remains a significant global health challenge, demanding a deeper understanding of the molecular mechanisms governing its progression. Emerging evidence highlights the intricate involvement of the ghrelin system— comprising the ghrelin hormone, its receptor (growth hormone secretagogue receptor, GHS-R), and associated signaling pathways—in modulating CRC biology. This review synthesizes current literature concerning the multifaceted role of the ghrelin system in CRC, from its contribution to colon carcinogenesis to its potential as a diagnostic and prognostic biomarker. We explore the mechanisms by which ghrelin and GHS-R activation influence key processes driving CRC advancement, including cell proliferation, angiogenesis, metastasis, and chemoresistance. Furthermore, we analyze the paradoxical nature of the ghrelin system, noting its context-dependent effects; while often promoting tumor growth and survival in established CRC, ghrelin may also exhibit protective roles in early stages of colon carcinogenesis.  Finally, this review evaluates the therapeutic implications of targeting the ghrelin system in CRC, examining both strategies aimed at inhibiting ghrelin signaling and those leveraging its potential for improved drug delivery or modulation of the tumor microenvironment.  Future research directions focused on delineating the precise signaling cascades and identifying patient-specific responses to ghrelin-based therapies are crucial for translating these findings into effective clinical interventions.  Understanding the nuanced role of the ghrelin system offers a promising avenue for advancing CRC diagnostics, prognostics, and targeted therapeutics.
