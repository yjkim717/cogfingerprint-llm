**Abstract: Theoretical Modeling of Gold Nanoparticle Aggregation within Endosomal Compartments via Smoluchowski Dynamics**

Understanding the intracellular behavior of nanoparticles is crucial for optimizing their therapeutic and diagnostic applications. While experimental characterization of nanoparticle distributions is well-established, a comprehensive theoretical framework connecting microscopic dynamics to macroscopic observations remains a challenge. This study investigates the aggregation kinetics of gold nanoparticles (AuNPs) within cellular endosomes, employing a Smoluchowski equation-based model to predict cluster formation. We hypothesize that endosomal viscosity and surface charge influence aggregation rates. Our model incorporates a spatially-averaged diffusion approximation and accounts for Brownian motion and inter-particle interactions. Validation against quantitative microscopy data of AuNP distributions in cultured HeLa cells demonstrates a robust correlation between predicted cluster densities and experimentally observed values. Specifically, we observe a non-linear relationship between AuNP concentration and the average cluster size, consistent with diffusion-limited aggregation. The results provide a theoretical foundation for interpreting and predicting AuNP behavior in complex biological environments, informing future nanoparticle design and delivery strategies.  Sensitivity analysis reveals the critical role of endosomal viscosity in regulating cluster formation, suggesting potential avenues for manipulating intracellular nanoparticle organization.
