The MIREN protocol establishes consistent site selection criteria, standardized vegetation survey techniques (including plot size, sampling frequency, and data recording methods), and a common data management framework.  This standardization allows for direct comparisons of species distribution shifts across different mountain ranges and over time, mitigating a key limitation of previous studies.

Furthermore, the MIREN framework is explicitly designed to accommodate the detection and monitoring of invasive species, recognizing the increasingly significant role of biological invasions in altering mountain plant communities. We highlight the protocol’s adaptability to diverse mountain environments and its potential to contribute to early warning systems for climate-induced biodiversity loss. By promoting data interoperability and facilitating meta-analyses, MIREN aims to enhance our ability to predict future range shifts, prioritize conservation actions, and ultimately preserve the unique biodiversity of mountain ecosystems in the face of accelerating global change.  Implementation of this protocol represents a critical step towards a more unified and effective approach to mountain biodiversity monitoring and conservation in the 21st century.
