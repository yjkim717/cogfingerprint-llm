**Spatial Assessment of Immune Microenvironment Heterogeneity Predicts Prognosis in Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer via the SpatialVizScore**

The tumor microenvironment (TME) plays a critical role in cancer progression and response to therapy, with immune cell infiltration representing a key modulator. While traditional methods assess immune infiltration globally, they often fail to capture the spatial complexity and heterogeneity within the TME.  Here, we introduce the SpatialVizScore, a novel prognostic biomarker derived from multiplex protein imaging (MPI) of lung tumor tissue sections.  MPI allows for the simultaneous detection and quantification of multiple immune cell markers, enabling the characterization of spatially distinct immune populations and their relationships to tumor cells. Our method employs a computational algorithm to integrate these high-dimensional spatial data, generating a single, quantitative score (SpatialVizScore) reflecting the degree of spatially organized immune infiltration.  Applying this approach to a cohort of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) patients, we demonstrate a significant correlation between the SpatialVizScore and overall survival.  Specifically, tumors exhibiting a high SpatialVizScore, indicative of a spatially complex and organized immune infiltrate, were associated with improved patient outcomes. These findings highlight the importance of spatial context in assessing immune responses and suggest that the SpatialVizScore represents a promising tool for refining risk stratification, predicting therapeutic response, and potentially guiding personalized immunotherapy strategies in NSCLC. Future work will focus on expanding the biomarker panel and validating these findings across diverse cancer types.
