**The Ghrelin System: Emerging Roles in Colorectal Cancer Diagnostics, Prognostics, and Therapeutic Intervention**

**Abstract**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) remains a significant global health challenge, necessitating the exploration of novel biomarkers and therapeutic targets. Recent investigations have increasingly implicated the ghrelin system, encompassing ghrelin, ghrelin receptors (ghs-Rs), and modulating peptides, in the pathogenesis and progression of CRC. This review synthesizes current literature examining the multifaceted role of the ghrelin system within the context of colon carcinogenesis. We explore evidence demonstrating altered ghrelin and ghs-R expression profiles in CRC tissues compared to healthy controls, suggesting potential utility as diagnostic and prognostic indicators. Specifically, studies indicate a correlation between elevated ghrelin levels and advanced tumor stage, poorer differentiation, and reduced overall survival in CRC patients.  Furthermore, the pro-tumorigenic effects of ghrelin, mediated primarily through ghs-R activation, are discussed, encompassing enhanced cell proliferation, angiogenesis, and metastasis. Conversely, emerging preclinical data highlight the therapeutic potential of targeting the ghrelin system, including the development of ghs-R antagonists and strategies to modulate ghrelin secretion. While promising, the clinical translation of these approaches requires further investigation, particularly concerning the heterogeneity of ghs-R expression and the potential for off-target effects. Future research should focus on identifying specific patient populations most likely to benefit from ghrelin-targeted therapies and elucidating the complex interplay between the ghrelin system and other oncogenic pathways in CRC.
