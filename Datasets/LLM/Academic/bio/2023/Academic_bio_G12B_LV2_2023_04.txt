**Direct Observation of Procedural Skills (DOPS) as a Formative Assessment Tool in Obstetrics and Gynecology Postgraduate Training: A Mixed-Methods Exploration**

**Abstract**

The effective evaluation of procedural competence is a cornerstone of postgraduate medical training, particularly within the technically demanding field of Obstetrics and Gynecology (OBGY). Traditional assessment methods often struggle to accurately gauge skill acquisition and identify areas for targeted improvement. This study investigates the feasibility and perceived utility of Direct Observation of Procedural Skills (DOPS) as a formative assessment tool within an OBGY postgraduate training program. Utilizing a mixed-methods approach, we assessed the experiences of both trainees and faculty members regarding the implementation of a structured checklist-based DOPS system. Data were collected through post-observation questionnaires assessing perceptions of assessment validity, utility for feedback, and impact on procedural performance, supplemented by qualitative focus group discussions exploring nuanced perspectives on the process. Quantitative analysis revealed consistently positive perceptions across both trainee and faculty groups regarding the clarity and relevance of the structured checklist.  A significant proportion of participants (78% of trainees and 85% of faculty) reported that DOPS facilitated more targeted and actionable feedback compared to traditional assessment methods. Qualitative data underscored the value of real-time observation in identifying both strengths and weaknesses in procedural technique, and highlighted the potential of DOPS to foster a culture of continuous improvement. While the study demonstrates promising initial results, limitations include the reliance on self-reported data and the absence of a comparator group using alternative assessment strategies. Future research should focus on incorporating objective performance metrics alongside subjective feedback, exploring the longitudinal impact of DOPS on procedural skill development and patient outcomes, and investigating the scalability and generalizability of this approach to diverse OBGY training settings. Furthermore, exploring the integration of DOPS with simulation-based training may offer a comprehensive approach to procedural skill mastery.

**Keywords:** Direct Observation of Procedural Skills, Formative Assessment, Obstetrics and Gynecology, Postgraduate Training, Structured Checklist, Medical Education, Competency-Based Education.
