**Abstract: Tantalum Pentoxide Dielectric Metasurfaces for Broadband Ultraviolet and Visible Optical Manipulation**

The burgeoning field of metasurface optics demands materials exhibiting high refractive index contrast, low loss, and robust fabrication compatibility across a broad spectral range. While noble metal-based metasurfaces have demonstrated significant functionality, their inherent optical losses, particularly at shorter wavelengths, limit performance and scalability. This work investigates tantalum pentoxide (Ta₂O₅) as a promising alternative dielectric material platform for realizing high-performance metasurfaces operating in the ultraviolet (UV) and visible regions of the electromagnetic spectrum.  Ta₂O₅ possesses a comparatively high refractive index (n ≈ 2.1-2.3) and intrinsically low absorption, enabling the design of efficient and broadband optical elements.

We present a comprehensive theoretical and experimental study of Ta₂O₅ dielectric metasurfaces, employing rigorous finite-difference time-domain (FDTD) simulations to optimize unit cell geometries for various functionalities, including beam steering and polarization conversion. Fabrication was achieved via reactive ion etching (RIE) on thin-film Ta₂O₅ deposited by atomic layer deposition (ALD), ensuring precise feature definition and minimizing material damage.  Experimental results demonstrate efficient operation of fabricated metasurfaces exhibiting polarization-dependent beam deflection across the visible spectrum, with observed efficiencies comparable to, and in some cases exceeding, those reported for conventional noble metal designs.  Furthermore, preliminary UV characterization reveals promising performance extending into the deep-UV regime.

This research establishes Ta₂O₅ as a viable and compelling candidate for broadband dielectric metasurface applications, offering advantages in terms of reduced optical losses and enhanced fabrication control. Future work will focus on exploring more complex metasurface designs, investigating the material’s performance under high-intensity illumination, and integrating these devices into functional optical systems for applications in areas such as microscopy, sensing, and advanced displays. The adaptability of ALD and RIE processes also allows for the facile integration of Ta₂O₅ metasurfaces with existing microfabrication workflows.
