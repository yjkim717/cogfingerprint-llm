**Abstract**

The holographic correspondence posits a duality between gravitational theories in *d* dimensions and conformal field theories (CFTs) residing on their boundary. A critical test of this duality involves understanding the fate of black hole microstates, particularly the potential for transitions between black hole geometries and highly excited string states within the underlying theory. This work investigates the dynamics of such transitions in the context of both heterotic and Type II superstring theories, employing a sigma model approach to analyze black hole geometries and their relation to string excitation spectra. Our analysis reveals a marked distinction in the behavior of these transitions between the two string theory realizations. Specifically, we demonstrate that for heterotic string theory, the transition from a small black hole to a highly excited string configuration exhibits a suppression of tunneling amplitudes, indicative of a robust geometric barrier. Conversely, in Type II superstring theories, the transition is characterized by a comparatively weaker suppression, suggesting a greater propensity for quantum tunneling and a potentially more complex landscape of string states accessible from the black hole horizon. These contrasting behaviors offer crucial insights into the non-perturbative structure of string theory and provide a stringent test of the holographic principle, particularly concerning the quantum microstates of black holes. Further investigation into the implications for AdS/CFT correspondence is warranted.
