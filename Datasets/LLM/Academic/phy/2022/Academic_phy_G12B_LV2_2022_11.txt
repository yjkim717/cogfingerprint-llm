**Abstract**

We present a novel approach to constructing and verifying holographic models of stellar motion within asymptotically Anti-de Sitter (AdS) spacetimes. Leveraging the AdS/CFT correspondence, we demonstrate a specific source configuration in the dual conformal field theory (CFT) that generates a gravitational background supporting stable, orbiting stellar trajectories. This construction utilizes insights from asymptotically AdS spacetimes, building upon recent developments in understanding holographic descriptions of compact astrophysical systems. Our method allows for the precise prediction of stellar orbits based on the properties of the dual quantum field theory, offering a unique avenue for testing the validity of the AdS/CFT correspondence in a regime relevant to astrophysical observations.  We detail the mapping between CFT source parameters and resulting stellar orbital characteristics, including orbital period and precession. Further investigation explores the potential for extending this framework to incorporate more complex astrophysical scenarios, such as black hole binaries, and to examine the effects of quantum field theory fluctuations on stellar dynamics. This work provides a concrete realization of holographic stellar dynamics and opens new perspectives on the interplay between general relativity and quantum field theory.
