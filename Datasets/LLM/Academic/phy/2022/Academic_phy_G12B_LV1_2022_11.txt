**Abstract**

The holographic duality, specifically the AdS/CFT correspondence, provides a powerful theoretical framework for exploring gravitational phenomena through the lens of quantum field theory. This work investigates the feasibility of simulating bounded stellar orbits within asymptotically Anti-de Sitter (AdS) spacetimes using techniques derived from quantum field theory computations. We present a novel approach leveraging the duality to construct a boundary condition for a scalar field on the AdS boundary, such that its bulk evolution corresponds to the geodesic motion of a massive test particle—effectively, an orbiting star—in the AdS interior. This methodology allows for the direct computation of orbital parameters and stability through perturbative analysis of the dual field theory.  Preliminary results suggest that well-controlled stellar orbits can be realized with appropriate choices of boundary conditions, providing a potential avenue for studying astrophysical scenarios analogous to those found within AdS/CFT. The implications for understanding black hole dynamics and gravitational wave signatures in strongly coupled systems are discussed, alongside considerations for extending this approach to more complex gravitational backgrounds.
