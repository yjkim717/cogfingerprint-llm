**A J-Band Tip-of-the-Red-Giant Branch (TRGB) Calibration Using Detached Eclipsing Binaries in the Large Magellanic Cloud and its Application to Extragalactic Distance Determination**

The accurate determination of extragalactic distances remains a fundamental challenge in modern astrophysics. While various distance ladder techniques exist, the Tip-of-the-Red-Giant Branch (TRGB) method, relying on the predictable luminosity of red giant stars at the end of their evolution, offers a compelling, relatively model-independent approach. This study presents a refined calibration of the J-band TRGB luminosity for asymptotic giant branch (AGB) stars within the Large Magellanic Cloud (LMC). We leverage a sample of newly identified, well-characterized detached eclipsing binary systems in the LMC to provide an independent and precise geometric distance measurement. These binaries serve as crucial anchors, circumventing systematic uncertainties inherent in traditional photometric calibrations.

By comparing the observed J-band magnitudes of TRGB stars with the binary-derived LMC distance, we establish a robust luminosity-metallicity relation for the TRGB. This calibration is then applied to estimate distances to nearby galaxies, specifically targeting NGC 253. Our results demonstrate excellent agreement with previously published TRGB distances, validating the consistency of this methodology across different datasets.  Furthermore, the improved J-band calibration presented here offers advantages in environments with significant dust extinction, where near-infrared observations are less affected.  This work underscores the power of combining high-precision binary distance measurements with established stellar evolution techniques for refining the extragalactic distance scale and contributing to a more accurate understanding of the cosmic distance ladder.
