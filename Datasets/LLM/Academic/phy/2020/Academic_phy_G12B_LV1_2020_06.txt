**Quantum Horizon Dynamics and the Resolution of Black Hole Singularities: A Loop Quantum Gravity Inspired Perspective**

The long-standing paradox of black hole evaporation, wherein Hawking’s semi-classical calculations predict thermal radiation and eventual complete decay while simultaneously implying the persistence of a spacetime singularity, motivates a deeper investigation into the quantum nature of black hole horizons. This work explores a framework incorporating tenets of Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG) to address these conceptual tensions. We propose that the event horizon, traditionally viewed as a sharp boundary, undergoes a quantum dynamical evolution at the Planck scale, potentially exhibiting granular structure and non-trivial topology. Utilizing a modified semi-classical gravity approach informed by LQG's background-independent quantization of geometry, we investigate the emergence of effective horizon geometries that soften or even resolve the classical singularity.  Specifically, we examine the influence of quantum geometry fluctuations on the stress-energy tensor near the horizon, and their impact on the Hawking radiation spectrum.  Our preliminary results suggest a deviation from the standard thermal spectrum, potentially encoding information about the black hole's initial state and offering a pathway towards a consistent quantum description of black hole evaporation, avoiding the problematic formation of a true singularity. Further investigation into the connection between horizon entropy and LQG’s area spectrum is ongoing.
