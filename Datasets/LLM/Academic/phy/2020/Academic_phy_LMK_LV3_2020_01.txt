Title: Deterministic Quantum Mechanics: Unveiling the Interplay between Locality and Classical Evolution Laws

Abstract:
This study investigates the mathematical conditions under which a quantum system can be rendered equivalent to a deterministic system, focusing on the intricate role of locality and interactions. By examining the Hamiltonian formulation of quantum systems, we establish a framework that reconciles the principles of deterministic quantum mechanics with classical evolution laws. Our analysis reveals that locality plays a crucial role in determining the conditions for a quantum system to exhibit deterministic behavior. We demonstrate that, under specific constraints on the Hamiltonian, a quantum system can be mapped onto a classical system, adhering to deterministic evolution laws. The findings of this research have significant implications for our understanding of the interplay between quantum mechanics and classical determinism, shedding new light on the fundamental principles governing the behavior of physical systems. Published in 2020.
