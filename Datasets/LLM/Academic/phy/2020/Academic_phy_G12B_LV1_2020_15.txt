**Abstract: γ-Lithium-Rich Chromium-Silicate Vanadate (γ-LCSVO) as a Zero-Strain Anode Material for Enhanced Lithium-Ion Battery Performance**

The relentless pursuit of high-energy-density lithium-ion batteries (LIBs) necessitates exploration of advanced anode materials capable of accommodating substantial lithium intercalation while exhibiting exceptional structural integrity. A significant challenge in utilizing lithium-rich layered oxides is their substantial volume expansion during cycling, leading to capacity fade and diminished lifespan. This work introduces γ-LCSVO (Li<sub>3.08</sub>Cr<sub>0.02</sub>Si<sub>0.09</sub>V<sub>0.9</sub>O<sub>4</sub>), a novel ternary oxide composition designed to mitigate this volumetric instability.  Through a combination of X-ray diffraction (XRD), scanning electron microscopy (SEM), and electrochemical characterization, we demonstrate that γ-LCSVO exhibits a remarkably “zero-strain” behavior throughout repeated lithium intercalation/deintercalation cycles.

The incorporation of chromium and silicon dopants into the vanadium-based framework strategically stabilizes the lattice, suppressing the characteristic volume changes observed in conventional lithium-rich oxides. Electrochemical testing, employing a half-cell configuration with a lithium metal counter electrode, reveals a high initial specific capacity of 360 mAh g<sup>-1</sup> and, crucially, demonstrates excellent capacity retention, retaining 85% of its initial capacity after 500 cycles at a current density of 1 C.  Differential capacity analysis (DCA) suggests a predominantly single phase transformation during lithiation, further supporting the structural stability hypothesis.  Furthermore, impedance spectroscopy confirms a stable charge transfer resistance throughout cycling, indicative of minimal electrode degradation. These findings establish γ-LCSVO as a promising anode candidate for next-generation LIBs, offering a pathway towards high-performance energy storage devices with improved cycle life and operational stability, addressing a critical bottleneck in widespread LIB adoption. Future work will focus on full-cell battery fabrication and optimization of electrolyte compatibility.
